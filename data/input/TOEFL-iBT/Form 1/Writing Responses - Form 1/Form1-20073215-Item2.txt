My parents have always encouraged me to study what really interests me, and I really agree that people should choose to study subjects they feel interesting.
   By saying this, I don't want to underestimate the importance of learning skills and acquire knowledge which can be later used for a job or career. But often, we, and the people who might help us in our choices, are not able to know what kind of subjects will be appriciated when we will begin our job search. 
   For example, in the past, it was believed that computer science was definitely the most useful subject to study to find a good job. But when the whole web-economy showed its deficencies and many computer science industries failed, in the late 90s, a lot of people with a computer science degree were fired and students who had previoulsy chosen it as a major could not easily find a job any more. And this is only an example of how difficult it might be to know beforehand which subject prepares you the best for a job.
    On the contrary, I think that studing what you really like is the only way to perform well in what you do. For instance, I really think it is the only way to graduate from university with good grades, and hence this choice also enhances your chances of being taken into a good graduate school which might allow you to write a better cv, and to get a good job as well. So, indirectly, if you choose to study a subject you like, you might increase the chances of having a brilliant career. 
   Another reason which is pretty convincing, is that, when choosing to study something only thinking about your chances of getting a job, you are not considering the fact that you might end up doing something that you don't like for the rest of your life. And I don't think many people don't feel excited by this thought.
