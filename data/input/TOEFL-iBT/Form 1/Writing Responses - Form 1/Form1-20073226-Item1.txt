  Three theories have been stated about how the birds get their ability to navigate while migrating. 
  Each of these theories is incomplete by itself, but the three of them together would form a complete explanation. One of the stated theories was that birds depended on celestial objects such as the Sun and stars. It was explained by saying that birds migrating in the daylight depend on the Sun, while birds migrating at night depend on the stars. This explanation is incomplete, because many times the Sun and the stars are invisible because of clouds, and yet the birds still figure out their way and make it through their journey.
   The second theory sais that birds use landmarks they already know to help them find their ways. But this theory is also incomplete because a study has been made where birds have been taken to a new location and were left to fly and were able to go back to their nests.
   The third and last theory suggests that birds have a kind of an internal compass on which they depend to find their way. This theory is still not enough, because even though the bird is able to tell south from north, but if the bird does not know where it is originally or how far it has to fly, then what is the use of this internal compass.
