All the theories that attempt to explain the ability of birds to migrate  are partial explainations. None can give a difinite explaination for birds' navigational capabilities.

The first thoery suggests that the birds navigate in reference to celestial objects like the sun or the stars. The limitataion of this thoery is that neither the sun nor the stars are visible all the time due to the cloud cover. Yet the birds manage to migrate accurately.

The second theory claims that the birds navigate by landmarks like rivers, coastlins, mountains etc. This theory is also not completely true, because there have been studies, in which the birds have been taken to places they have never been to before and it has been seen that they navigate back to their correct destinations.  If the birds navigated by landmarks, the above result would not have been possible. 

The third theory claims that birds use 'in built' magets that respond to the earth's magntic field.  This can not be accepted as the sole reason for the birds' ability to migrate, simply because, the knowldge of the direction- north or south- is not enough for migration to an exact location. The birds need to know where they are located and how far they are from their destinations. These questions are not answered by the above theory.
