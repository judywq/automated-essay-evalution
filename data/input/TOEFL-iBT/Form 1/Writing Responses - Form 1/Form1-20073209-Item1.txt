In the lecture points were raised that explained the limitations of the three threories in the reading passage. The points mentioned are:

    Firstly, birds migrate even when the sun and stars are not visible. As result the theory of celestial navigation is completely not true; it is partially true. It is true that birds use the sun and stars to migrate but in the absence of the sun and the stars especially on days when the sun and stars are not visible, birds will still migrate.

     Secondly, birds will find their way home even when they find themselves in a strange place. The theory put forward that birds memorize landmarks in other to be able to migrate also fall short of completeness. In that birds will find their way home even when they do not know their environment.  There has been cases in which birds have been taking to strange places to see if they could find their home and in all cases or at least in most  cases, the birds find their home.

    Thirdly, birds would have to know where they are in order to know where they are going. Even though birds used the earth's magnetic field to migrate, in order for them to be successful in using earth's magnetic field they must first know where they are, which their internal compass can not determine.

     In concluding the three theories when put togethter is complete but when look at individually they are incomplete. Therefore a holistic approach must be taken for the three theories to hold completely.
