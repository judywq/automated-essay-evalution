Subjects you should choose in your professional career.

Usually people decide to study something (college, post graduate) because they like it, whatever it is they like it. That's the first step to decide their professional future. An in a positive scenario they will work on something related to their previous studies.  

When people is at school they should choose subjects which combine their personal and professional preferences, thats the only way the may become the best ones at whatever they are doing. 

I recall that when I was at law school and had the option to choose some subjects, I picked subjects as bankruptcy law, legal aviation matters and law and economics, which I'd really enjoyed.Also my classmates picked their subjects (i.e. criminal law, intelectual property law, labor law proceeding) because they were interested in them. The result was that optional subjects were the ones in which you could ask and hear very interesting types of questions from people that appreciate a professor specialist in that area. Even when I had to study for final exams or prepare projects, it was very interest to discover new things and analyzed different points of views.

Therefore,  now I practice law which involves cases as the ones contained in the subjects that I picked at school. I'm very happy and one day I want to teach aviation legal matters and really expect to have my students involved in every moment of the class.

I can conclude that not every time you can choose the subjects to be studied in the professional program yo decide to take, but if you have the possibility to do it, the best thing you can do is to take the subjects in which you are more interested, enjoyed them and reflect the acquired knwoledge in you professional carrer.
