There are 3 main theories on how birds navigate to certain locations on earth, however each theory has its drawbacks. 
The firs theory is that birds navigate using celestial objects like the sun or stars. However this could not be their only method of navigation, as these objects aren't always visible. During the day clouds could block the view of the sun, and light polution from cities could block the bird's sight on the stars during the night, yet they still manage to find their way. 
The second theory is that the bird navigates using it's memory of land marks such as rivers, seas, and mountains. However this would mean that if a bird is released in an new surrounding it wouldn't find it's way back, as the visual image would not correspond with its memory. Yet research has shown that birds will find their way back.
The last theory states that embedded in the birds beak is a chemical called Magnetite which as the name says is magnetic, and reacts to the earth's magnetic field. Therefore the bird is able to locate North, South, East and West. However just knowing the bearing is not going to get a bird to a specific location, as it needs to know how far away the location is.
