      In the first theory the sun and stars are pointed out as the basic elements of  finding the original root for migration of birds. Although  birds  may use celestical  objects, they  easily find their ways when it is raining or  cloudy. As a matter of fact birds can find their correct direction when the sun and stares are invisible. 
     The second theory is  talking about landmarks  and its usage in finding  the right path by birds. Since the birds can find their correct way  when they are  in an unknow area, this suggestion can not  work properly.
     The last  theory is  the internal compass of the birds. It could  be very usefull but  can not answer some questions. For example how can they find their distances  from the final point of the trip.
