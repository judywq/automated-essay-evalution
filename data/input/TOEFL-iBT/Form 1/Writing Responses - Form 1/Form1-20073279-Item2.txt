The question of choosing the right subject to study and planning your career and life has become more and more important during the last few decades. Unemployment and globalization have scared people and newspaper articles and statistics on young people unable to find jobs make you wonder what is the path to happiness: assuring yourself a financially stable future by choosing a subject according to job perspectives or studying what you are interested in and like to do?
The question is hard to answer because nowadays even academics with a college degree in science, law or medicine can end up in working in callcenters struggling to make a living.
And the pressure is even greater: Everywhere you go you are told that you have to belong to the best, or else there is no chance for you to find a job.
Now, all this sounds very pessimistic. However, in my opinion it is not.
I believe that the key is to find something you are good, or very good, or one of the best at. And the things you are good at are, in most cases, the things you like doing. 
If people have fun studying the subjects they have chosen, they will be good at it. 
On the other hand, if they always have in mind that they are only studying law because there is a fair chance to get a decent, well-paid job afterwards, while in fact they have always wanted to be a graphic designer or a primary school teacher, they will not try hard enough, not get good grades and be unhappy.
I believe that both questions, whether a subject prepares you for a job or career and whether you like the subject, go hand in hand.
Employers will look for employees who love their jobs and like to work hard because they enjoy what they are doing.
Putting it in a nutshell, choosing to study a subject which you are interested in and which you like will, in my opinion, prepare you for a job or career. And, this is the good news: It will prepare you for that one job or career you want for yourself.
