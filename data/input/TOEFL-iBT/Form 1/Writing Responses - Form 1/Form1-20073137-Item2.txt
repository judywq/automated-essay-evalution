I totally agree with the statement. I think is more important to choose to study objects you are interested in and these are a few reasons that will help me to back up my answer.

The first and to me the main reason is because you will be driven by passion. For example, a person that became a lawyer because the possibility of making good money may be happy with what he/she does but the man that chose dancing or painting as his profession because he loved it, will be giving his best (his heart) at any training session or at any presentation because that is clearly what his mind and body wanted to be.

The second reason would be that when you study things that are related to what you like, you find studying interesting and enjoyable and it does not matter if you have to dedicate long hours to do it, you will feel that it is worth doing it. When you have to study  just to pass an exam or for example to get some credits, studying sessions are long and boring and the material you study is not memorized easily.

The final reason will be given through an example. There are many people that choose to study careers because the possibility of making good money or because the job market has lots of possibilities but not because those are things they are really interested in. In my experience these are the ones that after being involved for ten or fifteen years doing something they did not loved, will end up quiting their jobs and trying to find a real meaning to their lifes and wondering why did they let money and vanity plan their futures.

To summarize, when you let passion and not money to be the driving power in your life, complete satisfaction at the end of the journey will be guaranteed.
