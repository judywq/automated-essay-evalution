None of the three theories can't tell the whole story about bird's navigation. All theories make good points yet there are situations in bird navigation they cannot explain. The first theory about celestial objects e.g. can't explain how birds manage the difficulty of navigating when the sun resp. the stars are not visible. Clowds obscuring the celestial objects is not an unusual weather situation. Still, birds are able to navigate without that "map". The theory about navigation by landmarks is limited as well. What about a bird being unfamiliar with the landscape it is facing? Many studies have shown that birds find their way home even after being released in an environment they are unfamiliar with and even navigate their way back home. Finally, the theory which proposes birds using an internal compass responding to the Earth's magnetic field may be right in birds having mineral magnetites in their beaks. Nevertheless it doesn't meet the difficulties of a bird finding its way from a specific place to another. Since a "built-in compass" only shows the direction you're heading towords, it simply doesn't tell where one is.
