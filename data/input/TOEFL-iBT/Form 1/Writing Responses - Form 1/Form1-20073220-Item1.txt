The lecture states that all three of the theories are only "partial theories".
For the first theory which says that birds navigate depending on the sun or the stars, the lecture opposes by stating that the sun and stars are not always visible, and hence birds cannot navigate solely dependant on the position of the sun or stars.
The second theory states that the birds memorize landmarks to help them navigate. However, in the lecture it says that birds cannot travel just by memory, because that theory would not explain how birds can travel to their various destinations even when they are placed in a foreign location.
The third theory proposes tht birds travel through a magnetic field encompassed in their beaks to travel. While this theory may be true, it cannot solely rely on this because, although knowing North from South and East from West is useful, the exact position in which the bird starts its travel and also the distance in which it would have to travel in order to get to its destination is also relevant.
The lecture, in conclusion, says that although all three theories are plausible, they cannot be proposed independently.
