According to the lecturer, there is support for all three theories presented in the passage, but no single theory can on its own explain the whole phenomenon.

The first theory states that migrating birds use celestial objects like the sun or the stars to navigate. This cannot be the whole truth as the sun and the stars are not always visible and the birds cannot use them as their only navigation.

The second theory states that landmarks and a navigational memory are the reasons why birds find their way home.  However, experiments have shown that birds that are released in a new environment will find their way back home even though they have never seen the environment they are placed in. They can locate themselves even though no familiar surroundings are visible and this is not explained by the second theory in the passage.

An internal compass in the beak caused by magnetic magnetite is the third theory, but it does not either get full support as a compass does not help if the bird does not know where it is and where it is going. To head south does not mean the bird will hit the right destination.
