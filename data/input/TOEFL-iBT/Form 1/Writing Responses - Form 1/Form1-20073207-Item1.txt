Different theories have been presented in attempts to investigate the birds' unique navigational abilities. According to the lecturer, none of the theories explain the whole mechanism of birds' migration process. The lecturer provides several explanations which prove the incompleteness of these theories. For example,  the first theory is not perfect because the simple observation of celestical objects is not always possible. However when it's cloudy birds still manage to find their way. As for the second theory, which claims that birds navigate by landmarks, the lecturer gives a good example of birds put in an unknown location. Despite the new place, birds navigated their way back to the nests. A third theory suggests that birds have a type of internal compass. Nevertheless, in the lecturer's words the "built-in" compass doesn't count by itself. It is only able to give the orientation but not the right destination.
