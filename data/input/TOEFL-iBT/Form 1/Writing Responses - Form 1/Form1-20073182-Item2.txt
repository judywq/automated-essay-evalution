It is more important to choose to study subjects you are interested in than to choose subjects to prepare for a job or career. This is because you will tend to do better in something that appeals to you, that you find interesting, and that triggers your imagination and creativity when it comes to problem solving. 

In a job or career that is essentallly what happens; you will be presented different problems, most of which you have never been exposed to before, and you need to apply a combination of both learned and non-learned skills (this is where creativity plays a role) in order to arrive at a well-functioning solution. The problem arises if you are not affectionate enough about your job to see beyond what has been presented to you in textbooks. 

Still, many people are successful at choosing subjects that they are interested in and that also prepare them for a job or career, but the most weight should nevertheless be put on choosing what you like to do. It doesn't help if a career offers you lots of money if you aren't happy there and do not feel you are doing a good job, and it is even worse if  your colleges don't think you are doing a good job either. If you like physics, study physics, and later you will find that they need physicists in a variety of jobs, even the jobs you didn't think about when choosing the subject, and you will be successful at it because you love it.
