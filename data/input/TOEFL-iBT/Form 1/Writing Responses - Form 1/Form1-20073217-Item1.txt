There have been several theories explaining the navigation techniques used by birds. 
One suggested theory is that birds are guided by celestial objects while travelling long distances. Sun and its movements guide the birds during day time while positioning of stars serves the purpose of a map for nocturnal birds.
Second theory deals with the memorizing skills of the birds. The Hippocameral region of brain, responsible for the memory formation helps birds in identifying and memorizing different landmarks, coastlines and mountains which help birds in specifying their routes to desired location.
A third theory proposes that birds uses a type of internal compass that responds to Earth's magnetic field. It states that birds have pieces of magnetite on their beaks which acts an internal compass and hence help them in sensing the direction of pull of earth's magnetic field which further  helps them in finding their routes and paths.
These theories could at best be assumed as a partial explanation to birds navigation techniques. There are many instances which contradicts the completeness of mentioned theories like stars and sun are not always visible to birds especially on cloudy days and still they are able to find their way. Also, if released in an unknown place, birds are still able to come back and retrace their path. Lastly, presence of compass seems insufficient in calculation of distances,specific location etc. which are  imporatant tools required for navigation.
The best possible explanation to birds navigation techniques could be a combination of all three techniques used by birds in migrating through long distances.
