Although each theory has some support, the three theories presented in the reading can only be considered partial explanations. The first theory suggests that the birds navigate in reference to the sun and stars. However, the birds can also find the right direction when it is cloudy and they can't see the sun or the stars. According to the second theory, the birds navigate by landmarks by making use of memorization skills. There is evidence to support this theory, yet also birds that have been taken to a place to which they have never been before can find their way back home. The third theory claims that the birds' ability to navigate is due to a "built-in compass." They can sense the Earth's magnetic field, and use that to find their way. Still, this theory does not explain how the birds know how to find a particular place, i.e. exactly how far and in what direction to travel.
