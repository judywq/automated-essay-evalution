Reasons behind the ability of birds to migrate long distances, is the point of discussion in both the passage and the lecture. The passage puts forward three thories on how the birds manage to do this. The lecture mentions that the theories are not complete in their explanation of the bird's behaviour.

The first theory maintains that the birds navigate with the help of celestial bodies.
But this cannot wholly explain their behaviour, for the simple fact that, celestial bodies can be obscured at times.The sun and the stars are not visible always.They could be hidden by clouds. Yet the birds are able to navigate to their destination correctly, even in such conditions.

The second theory suggests that the birds naivgate by memorizing landmarks and keeping them as reference points.
If this were true, birds would not be able to travel back from places they haven't been to, before. But some researchers, conducted experiments in which birds were taken to previously unseen places, and then released. They successfully found their way home. Hence this also proves that this theory is not foolproof in it's explaination.

The third theory explains the navigation of birds by mentioning the presence of an internal compass that they possess. 
Even though this sounds far fetched, navigation cannot be done just by knowing the  direction in which to fly.The current location and the distance to the destination should also be known, for successfully navigating to a place.Hence this theory can also not account for bird navigation all by itself.
