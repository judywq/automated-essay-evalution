In the lecture, the professor talks about the limitations for each of the three theories which explain how could be understood the ability of migrating birds to accurately navigate extremely long journeys. 

The first theory is based upon the idea that the ability of birds to migrate is due to celestial bodies, stars. But limitations to this theory are explained because it is not possible to explain how can birds get to places; even though, the stars cannot be seen, for example how they manage to get places in a very cloudy day? 

The second  theory is related to the abilities of birds to migrate and this theory is founded on the premise that birds memorize landmarks such as mountains, rivers and coastlines. Also, the professor says this theory is limited because it fails in explaining why birds can reach the accurate places if they have never been on that route or in that place, which is very common, birds get to places where they have never been before.

The third theory trys to explain that birds gets to the places because they have an internal compass, which guides them through a magnetic mechanims that sistematically re-allocate their routes in order to reach an specific place (migration place). The professor also says that this theory is not really accurate in expplaining the phenomenom because birds can get to places where the mechanism would have experienced some problems because there is not sufficiet magnetic attraction  forces to permit birds use their internal compass: thus, the conclusion is that they would get lost, but this is not the reality: birds reach places with any problem at all.  

To sum up, based on the three theories, the professor gives extra information in order to expose how they are limited and fails to explain in an accurate way the phenomenom.
