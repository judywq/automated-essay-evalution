I used to think that choosing to study subjects I am interested in was more important than to choose subjects to prepare for a job or a carrer. After all you just have to do it. It seems easy at the first glance. Nowadays my exprerience make me think that this statement is wrong.

First, nowadays doing what you want does not allow you to make a living. It is useless to study a subject if after your studies you find yourself unemployed. You will not even have the chance to use all the things you learned.

In addition, the economic environment is all the more ubiquitous since the emergence of the free market economy. Globalization has made the firms to choose their workers all over the world. They pick up the cheaper one in order to maximize their profit. That means that if you choose a competitive way you will only have two choices. Being unemployed or moving in poor countries in order to do what you like. It is likely that you will not be well payed but after all, you are bound to like your job, aren't you?

Besides, even if you choose to study a subject you like that will not means that you will like the job related to these kind of studies. For instance, you can study finance but in the end you will hate being an accountant. To some extent, interesting studies does not mean an interesting job.

In short, it is important to study a subject you are interested in but it is really more important to find a job. In my opinion, there is always a way to study an interesting subject which will make you construct a career. Obviously it is not always the most interesting topic but it could feet you to a large extent. 
There are so many problems we have to deal with when we graduate. We do not have to make the situation worst. Having a family and raising children is hard enought.
