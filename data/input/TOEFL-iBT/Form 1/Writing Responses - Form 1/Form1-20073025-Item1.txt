There is different theories to explain the ability of migrating birds to make long journeys.  
One of them, claims that birds navigate using the sun and the stars as a reference. Birds that migrate at day time, use the position of the sun in the sky; and birds that migrate at night time, use the stars and the North star as a reference. The limitation for this theory, is about the fact the is not always possible to see the sun and the stars. 
A second theory, suggests that migrating birds use rivers, lakes, coast lines, like references to locate themselves, using memorization skills to navigate.
