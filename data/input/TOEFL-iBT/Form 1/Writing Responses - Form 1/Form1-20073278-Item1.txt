     The lecture deals with the theories that have been propounded with their loop holes, to explain birds' navigational ablities.Although these theories are correct ,they are individually only  partial explanations.Hence, none of these individually explain the ability completely.
     The first theory suggests that birds navigate in reference to celestial objects,the sun at day and the stars at night. However, the sun and the stars are not always visible. They are sometimes obscure . However, this does not deter the navigation of the birds;hence,an incomplete theory.
     The second theory claims that birds travel by memorising landmarks. Owing to the hippocampal region in their brain they form memories that help them navigate. Contradicting this is the finding that birds navigate to their destination,even when taken to an unknown place.This is contradicting because their memory cannot produce information about unknown places. Hence, the theory is partial.
  The third theory states that birds have an inbuilt compass in their beaks, in the form os magnetite crystals .This aligns itself with the earth's magnetic field. However, just having a compass is not enough. In practise,we have seen that even though one might have a compass, we need information about things like, distance from the destination and where we are. Hence,this is also not a full theory.
