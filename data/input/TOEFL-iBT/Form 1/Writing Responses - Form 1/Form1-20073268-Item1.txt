     This lecture deals with the subject of birds remarkable abilities to navigate over long distances and journeys. Concerning this matter, three theories have been exposed in the lecture.

     The first theory claims that birds rely on the celestial objects, mostly the sun and stars, to navigate. Assumabily, birds would take the north star and the east-west path of sun as navigating references. This could be true, but then wouldn't be enough to explain birds ability to travel in cloudy conditions where no celestial reference would be available.

    The second explanation supposes that birds orient themselves using natural landmarks such as rivers and mountains thus emphasing the role of memory in birds' navigation. This theory is backed up by the fact that a damaged hippocampal region (a major memory center) would make a bird lose its navigating gifts. Nevertheless, this explanation doesn't explain how a bird introduced to a totally new location can easily find his way back home.

   The third theory states that a navigating bird relies on earth magnetic field to find his way. Indeed, a bird would use magnetite crystals found in his beak as an internal compass and navigate accordingly. However, in any given situation a compass alone is never enough to locate oneself. Capital informations such the original position and the forecasted heading are essential to navigate. That's why, this answer too does not gives a complete solution.

  In conclusion, none of the given theories can stand alone as an answer to the scientists wonderings. Every one of these gives a partial solution to the problem. Every one of these has a flaw or a weak point and needs to be completed.
