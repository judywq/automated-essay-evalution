Methods used by birds when migrating for long distances from one place to the other are three kinds. The first method is using the Sun and Stars (Celestial objects) as a reference to navigate from one place to the other, but in bad weather circumstances, neither of the above mentioned objects are available for navigation. The second method is memorizing landmarks, like rivers and mountains, but again, when birds are put in a completely new enviroment they tend to find their way successfully. Last method discussed is having a built-in compass and having crystals of the mineral magnetite embedded in their beaks,  which serves as a mechanism in detecting the north pole, but heading north or south is not sufficient enough nor does it provide a full and efficient way of navigating for birds. Birds use all the three methods explained above simultaneously to reach an optimum condition when flying and migrating from a country to the other.
