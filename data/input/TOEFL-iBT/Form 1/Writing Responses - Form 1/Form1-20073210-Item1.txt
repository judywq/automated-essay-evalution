The passage describes three possible explainations of the fact that the migarating birds are able to find their way back to their nests. The lecture, however suggests that all three of them are only a partial explaination of the phenomenon and do not explain the observed phenomenon completely.

The first theory suggests that the birds follow cretain celestial bodies like Sun or stars to find their way back to their nests, using them as references. By knowing the Sun's east/west path across the sky (for the birds those migrate in day) or the position of north star (in case the bird migrates in the night) can help them trace back their original path. The lecture raises a question on the completeness of this single theory for explaining the phenomenon by saying that the birds are able to find their way back even when the Sun and the stars are not visible, in case of cloudy or overcast sky.

The second theory suggest that the birds remember the way back by registering the various landmarks in their way into their hippocampal region of brain which is used to memorize. For eg if the bird encountered the rivers, coastlines or mountains, it traces back to find them and thereby finds way home. This according to the lecture is not a complete explaination. The lecture says that it is confirmed by the studies that even if the birds were left to new locations which they had not been to, they were able to find back their nests.

The third theory suggests that the birds have magnetite crystals embedded in their beaks which acts as an internal compass and helps the birds to find out the direction based on the Earth's magnetic pull on these magnetite crystals. The lecture again says that this cannot be the sole explaination for the bird's navitation as birds need to know other things apart from the direction viz where they are and how far they have to fly. Only the sense of direction would not be sufficient to take them back to their natural habitat.
