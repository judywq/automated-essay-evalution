The ability that birds have to know where they are going when they flight long distances for long time is still challenging scientists. Three different posibilities (theories) have been ascribed by them (scientists) in order to resolve the mystery.

The first theory states that birds guide themselves by looking at celestial objects (like the sun and the stars) and although it may be a valuable theory nobody clearly knows what happens when these celestial objects are covered by the clouds. 

The second theory states that their navigation system relies on landmarks (geographical landmarks) and that it is by following them (rivers, coasts and/or mountains) that they arrive to the destination the initially planned to arrive. Although is very accepatble and clearly understandable, nobody can explain how birds were able to arrive to the planned destination, after they left from places that were completely unknown by them.

And last but not least, the third theory mentions that Magnetite (a magnetic compund) located in the beaks and that responds to different signals produced by magnetic fields  on earth (working as an internal compass) help them to navigate.

Although all of them sound possible, non of them can explain by itself how migration/ navigation system works in birds. Probably it is a combiantion of these three systems, what allow birds to arrive to their destinations.
