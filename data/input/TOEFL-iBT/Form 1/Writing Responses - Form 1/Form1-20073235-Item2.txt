The subjects you choose to study are very important for your future because they will be useful for the rest of your life. So this makes this question arise "Is it more important to choose to study subjects you are interested in than to choose subjects to prepare for a job or career".

It is important to think about choosing subjects that will prepare you for a job or career because it will be what will make you a professional and expert on your field. For example, an architect must study subjects related to drawing and being accurate at it. It would be sensless if he or she just took subjects related to art. Or if you want to be a veterinarian, it would be usless to study human anatomy, even though it interests you.

On the other hand you should not study something you do not like. Therefore it is very important that you study subjects you are interested in, subjects that you know will be useful for you. For example, if you are studying nutrition and you have the chance of taking an eating disorders class or a sports health class. Both will prepare you for a career but you must choose what you like.

You must like the career you are studying or the job you are applying to, so there is no chance of having to decide between studying what interests you or what will prepare you. It is as simple as this, you are obviously not going to study art if it is not what interests you. 

The answer to the question stated above is that there is no need to choose because if you choose a career that interests you, probably all the courses that you will be taking will interest you.
