 I am sure that choosing subjects of interest  is more important than choosing subjects for a future career. Actually, I can prove this statement describing my own life-experience. 
 I have always been keen on art: painting, drawing and sculpturing. During my school years I have devoted most of my free time to this exciting hobby. My art-works have been praised and appreciated by friends and family. However, when there came a time to choose a major in the University my family insisted on picking the business administration. I had a year of an intensive preparatory courses and days of reading about economics, finance and other related boring subjects. Nevertheless, when there were two months left before the entrance exams I have suddenly changed my mind. I've decided to try myself in design major. The decision was very spontaneous and risky because there were only a couple of months left before the exams and there was no opportunity to get prepared appropriately in such a short time. Anyway, I have gathered all my amateur art works into portfolio and went to an admissions office. The most prodigious is that they have accepted me. 
 I have never in my life regreted the decision I have made. I am only on my last year in University and I already have an enviable for all interior-designers job in a world-famous company. I enjoy my work so much that I honestly barely ever stop working. I am doing work projects on the week-ends and during business-trips, I don't remember when I had a single day-off lately. Of course this is not because I lack time for doing my major obligements at work. Therefore, I am convinced that it is essentialy important to choose the things that you are passionate about in life in general, and subjects to study in particular. When you really like what you do and you devote yourself to the subject, you will definately succed in that area and make an amazing career out if it.
