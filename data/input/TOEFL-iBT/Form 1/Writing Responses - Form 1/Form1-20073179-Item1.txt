In these set of materials, the reading passage tells us three possible explainations why birds can identify the direction when they are migrating. The listening passage, however, don't agree with it, and the professor thinks that the explanations in the reading passage are all somewhat partial.

In the reading passage, the first explanation is that birds use celestial objects, such as the sun or stars, to lead their trip. In the listening passage, however, the professor believes that it is not reasonable. Because some evidences reveal that if the celestial objects are unvisible, such as much cloud appears in the sky, which hide the sun or stars, birds still can find their way to the destination.

The second theory in the reading passage seems also not correct. It is said that it is the landmarks navigate the birds. But in the listening passage, the professor said that landmarks are unnecessary for birds' migration. Because some studies show that if a bird is put in a unfamiliar environment, it will still find its nest or destination. 

The third theory is not reasonable, for it fail to explain the questions that how a bird find its way when the destination is far away and how to place when its flying begins. And the fact is that birds still can find its way even when they are in the situations mentioned above.
