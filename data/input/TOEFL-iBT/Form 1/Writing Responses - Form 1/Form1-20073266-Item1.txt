Both the reading and the talk discuss birds navigation. Whereas the reading focuses on three differents explanations, the talk highlights that each of these explanation is not sufficient by itself.

According to the reading there are three main reasons which could explain how birds navigate.
For some scientists birds use celestials objects such as the Sun or the stars in order to find their way.
For others memorization plays a major role in the birds orientation.
The last theory says that birds use an internal compass and the earth magnetic field in order to achieve their migration.

According to the lecture, all these theories are limited and should be taken all toghether.
Indeed, how could birds navigate at night if the sky is cloudy enought to prevent them from showing the stars?
And how birds could find a way to come back home if they have reached a new place?
In addition using an internal compass is not sufficient to navigate. After all, you have to master the distances to stop at your final destination.

In short, the reading may make valid points about birds navigation. Nevertheless, the talk give us some exemples which are really convincing. It shows us that all the theories are incompleted and do not deal with all of the situation. In other words, we have to take the three theories toghether in ordrer to have a more accurate explanation of this phenomenon.
