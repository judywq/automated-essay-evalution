There are three theories of bird's navigational abilities. The first one is based by the using of the celestial objects such as the sun and stars, but this theory is limitated for the reason that not always those objects are visible, they can be hidden by the clouds.
The second theory consists in the bird's navigational abilities by landmarks such as mountais and rivers, using them to find their way by tasting spatial abilities and memory; but also this theory is limitated by the fact that there are birds that go for the first time to a place, so, how can they have memory of a place never visited before? using this theory birds can also get lost.
And the last theory consists on that birds use a type of internal compass that can help them to find their way, but this theory is limitated by the fact that a compass is not enough to find a way, in this case the birds also need the memory to find out where they're going in order to find their destination.
