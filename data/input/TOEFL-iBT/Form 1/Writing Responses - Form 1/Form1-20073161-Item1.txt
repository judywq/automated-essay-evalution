To find their way from one point to another birds make use of memorzational skills.
There are three theories of how birds navigate from one point A to another point B.
One theory refers to the sun and stars birds focus on. They use the sun by day and the stars by night for orientation. The problem with this theory is that the sun and the stars are not visible every day. Clouds sometimes hide their vision.
A second theory is that birds navigate by landmark indication. Examples can be rivers, coastlines and mountains. The problem that occures is that if a bird really took its knowledge only out of this theory it could not be able to find its way back to the original point. It would get confused because of the opposite way of returning.
The third theory many naturalists discuss is that of an internal compass the birds make use of. The birds have crystals of the mineral magnetite embedded in their beaks which resposes to Earth's magnetic field.
