	The first theory refering to the methods used by birds in migration explains that the navigation abilites of birds are given by objects presented in the sky such as the Sun and stars.This theory is limited becasue birds do not necessarilly need a clear sky, meaning no clouds, to orient themselves from one place to another. This way, birds have migrated to places without a proper view and reference of either the Sun, or the stars.
	The second theory claims that birds have the ability to navigate accurately through their observation and memorization of landscapes. This theory is also a contradiction of what actually happnes because birds have been put in landscapes which are unfamiliar with them, and still, they have reached their destination. This way memory could not be the main role in the navigation methods of birds.
	The last theory suggests that birds have an internal compass which guides them in their migration. This is explained by the birds having crystals of magnetite, which is a magnetic substance, in their beaks that serves as a compass. This theory is also dismissed as purely correct because for a proper and accurate navigation, more than the direction of a compass is needed. To start, birds need an indication of where they are located to know where they need to be heading. Also, for proper travelling birds need to know the proper direction of where they specifically need to go, and a simple compass would not give them this information.
