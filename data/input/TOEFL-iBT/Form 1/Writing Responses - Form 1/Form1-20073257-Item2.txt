I have always believed that very few qusetions can be answered in a yes or a no. Therefore, I cannot fully agree or disagree with the given statement.

It is important to choose subjects that interest you, so that there is involvement and commitment in academics.  
At the same time we have to realise that we live in a material world. 
Fetching a job is very important, we will not be offered jobs unless we posses skills that are required of us. Formal training in the profession that is choosen may increase the chances of getting a good job. 

Then there is the third category. At times, people are genuinely interested in those subjects that are, incidentally, subjects that prepare you for a job. I am doing a course in Journalism, I love the subjects that the programme offers and, by default, it also prepares me for a profession that I aspire to be in. 

The problem arises when there is an imposition. In today's world, we see that more and more people are being pressurised to take up subjects that do not interest them, merely because the subjects are job-oriented. This is quite tragic. Because it is the arts and humanities that are being compromised upon. Management and the sciences are given more preferrence. But people have to realise that not everyone has an aptitude or interest for these subjects. 

Doctors, engineers and management professionals need specific traits and interests. If a person recognises that she is better at painting, then she should not take up a course in medicine merely because it pays her well. A good painter can earn well too. Also, she might turn out to be a bad doctor. 
But if a person is not able to make a decision on her career, then it is justified to choose the subjects that can prepare her for a job. So that her profession is secure. 

At the end, hard work in the choosen field is what matters.
