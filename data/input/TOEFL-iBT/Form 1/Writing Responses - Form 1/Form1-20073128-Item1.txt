For years, scientists have tried to explain the ability of migrating birds to accurately navigate extremely long journeys. There are three theories that are supported by  scientist, but none of them is completely right. 
Some people say that birds navigate in reference to celestial objects like the Sun or the stars. However, it is a contronversive statement. The Sun and the stars are not always visible for us or even birds becuase they are sometimes covered by cloud. Then, the second theory is that birds find the way by memorizing the landmarks. There is a experience that the scientists released the birds at a place, where the birds were not familiar with, but they still find their way home. It concludes that birds are not always dependent on the landmarks. Finally, the third one states that birds use a type of internal compass that responds to Earth's magnetic field. It is not quite reliable. Knowing the exact direction is very helpful for birds to finding their way home, but the direction can't point out  a specific location. Therefore, the third theory is also doubtful. 
In conclusion, although scientists have found some explainations, there still isn't any theory that can completely explain the phonomina. The ability of migrating birds to accurately navigate extremely long journeys is still a mistery.
