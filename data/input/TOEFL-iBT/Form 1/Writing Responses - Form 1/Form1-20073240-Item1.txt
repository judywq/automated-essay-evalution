The topic discussed in the lecture is  the ability of the birds to migrate and  some of the theories that are believed to  account for  their navigation have been discussed .However, all the theories have their certain limitations. 

The first theory explains that the birds navigate in refernce to celestial objects.But, this theory can not be accounted to all situations as these celestial bodies are not always visible.They might be hidden by clouds during winters suppose, but still the birds manage to migrate to respective destinations even in such weather.Hence, this theory is not completely reliable.

The second theory talks about the Hippocampal region of the brain which is associated with the memorization skills.But ,this also does not support compeltely, as birds do navigate to new places and they even return back to their home . So, the observation revelas that birds do respond to new places, and does not rely on memorized locations.

The third theory explaind  was that birds do have an internal compass that helps in migration.But, we can prove that compass is just not enough to move to different place as the birds should also have an idea about the source or the place they are at presently.Only when the source is known, that they can proceed towards their destination with the help of the Comapss they have.So, even this theory fails in accurately explaining the mechanisms birds use while migration.

Hence , it can be concluded that , there is no particular mechanism or a theory that suggests or supports the fact that birds accurately navigate very long distances . Navigational abilities of birds still remain as a mystery for various Naturalists and Ornithologists.
