The teacher indicates the main weak points of the three theories. Every theory tries to explain why birds are able to navigate so accurately. 

The first theory is not complete because the Sun and the Stars are not always visible, sometimes clouds or frog can make those elements invisible. so birds do not always have a reference to navigate.

The second theory is not complete because although birds rely on landmarks to navigate and their memory to remember these landmarks, if a bird is placed in an unknown place, it will find the way back home, although it can not recognize any landmark. For this reason, the ability of birds to remember landmarks and orientate is not enough, because birds can know where they are without any landmark or reference.

Finally, the third theory is very suugesting and could offer an explanation to the weak points of the two previous theories, but is also incomplete because birds' compass are not enough to navigate. The lecturer says that before the journeys, birds must know where they are and how long will lasts the journey, and this information is not provided by the internal compass. 

In conclusion, the lecturer has pointed why the three theories are not complete, but he has not suggested that the explanation of the accuracy of birds can be due to a combination of all the theories, because any of them is false or incorrect.
