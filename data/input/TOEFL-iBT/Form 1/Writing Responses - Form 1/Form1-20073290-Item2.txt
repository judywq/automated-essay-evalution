I agree with the statement that it's more important to study subjects you are interested in, rather than choose subjects that prepare you for a job or a career.
First of all I think you will do better in studying something that interests you, rather than something that will just make you money. This is because you have a lot of motivation and you will be eager to learn more about the subject, instead of just learning the cylabus. I also think it will make you more happy, as you enjoy what you are doing. 
There is also a very big drawback to choosing subjects preparing you for a job or career. Say you took al subjects you need, to become a farmer. Yet when you are finished with your studies you realise, there are allready to many farmers in your country,this means that you will be stuck with the subjects you took, which are all related to farming forcing you to stay in the agricultural business, as you don't have any qualifications to get employment in a different branch of work.
However if you took subjects related to farming because they interested you, it wouldn't be that bad, as any job in the agricultural business will be satisfactory as you are interested in it. 
A good example of being succesful simply because you enjoy what you are doing, are young students on the internet. An examlpe is the group of students that started youtube on www.youtube.com. Their main reason was because they liked the idea and wanted to do it. Only later on did they realise that it was becoming very succesful and commercially benefitial. This is an example of students that just did what they were best at. And often it is the case that you are best at that, that you enjoy doing most. It's the same with musicians. No musician has become a truelly great artist, because they chose music as they wanted to make money. They chose music because they liked it, and because they liked it, they became good at it, and so on. So your own interest is the greatest motivation you can have.
