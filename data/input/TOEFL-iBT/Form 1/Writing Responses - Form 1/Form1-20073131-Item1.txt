Birds in general navigate from one point to another based on several possibilities.

The reading identifies three of  these possibilities, however these three statements are limited by some evidences.

The first theory says that birds navigate following the Sun or the stars. But, this statement is not completly true. Some days the sun or the stars are not visible, that doesn't stop birds from navigating, they  still move on.

The second theory informs us that birds navigate using their memory. This conclusion cannot be completly verified. It may be possible but it doesn't stand for a complete truth. To show the limitation to this theory , the lecturer asks what will happen if a bird is placed in a place  it  has never been? Will it be able to go back home? The response is yes so the second theory is not affirmed.

Finally, the third theory indicates that birds move  in response to Earth magnetic field because they have a kind of internal  compass. To disagree with this point ,the lecturer says that for a compass to function correctly, one needs to know the starting point and how far is the final point.
