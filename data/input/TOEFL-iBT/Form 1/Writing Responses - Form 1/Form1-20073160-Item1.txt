The lecture presents the limitations for each of the explanations for the orientation of bird migration; none of them can independently explain the phonomenon.

The theory about the sun and the stars is not very reasonable for the lecture host; the sun and the stars do not always appear in the sky. The sun and the North star are merely visible in half of each day. Birds, however, are able to travel in both day and night.

The second theory states that birds can possibly remember the landmarks they have seen in their way. The lecture suggests that had the birds not known the new habbitat they were moved to, they are still able to find their way back to their nests.

The third theory involves the magnetic crystals in birds' beaks that might respond to the earth's magnetic field, therefore act as an internal compass for birds. Nevertheless, compasses do not show exact locations; birds cannot reach their nests only with the knowledge about direction.

The three theories have merely partially explained the phonomenon of bird migration.
