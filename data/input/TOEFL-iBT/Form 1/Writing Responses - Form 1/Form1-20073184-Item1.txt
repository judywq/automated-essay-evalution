The lecturer explains that there are support for each of the three theories that attempt to account for the birds' navigations abilities, but any of them can explain it entirely.
The first theory, which claimed that the birds are using the celestial objects to navigate, is limitated by the fact that birds migrate even when clouds are making this objects (the Sun or the stars) invisible.
The second theory is based on the landmarks. Because birds whose hippocampal region (used in memorization skills) have been damaged cannot find their way to migrate, it has been inferred that landmarks are remembered and used by the birds to navigate.
But the fact that some groups of birds are found in places in which they have never been before and whose location where unknown show that as the first one, this theory cannot account by itself for the birds' abilities.
The third theory assesses that birds use a type of internal compass located in their beaks and which indicates the north, thanks to the magnetic field. But it is a well-known fact that a compass is not enough to travel (one needs to know where the travels begins, and how long he has to travel). So this third theory can't be the whole explanation.
To conclude, the three methods of navigating presented above must be used together by the birds to migrate efficiently and to the right places.
