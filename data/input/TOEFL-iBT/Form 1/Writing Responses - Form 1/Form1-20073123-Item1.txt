  According to the professor, none of all theories can competely explain how birds can navigate themselves in migrating. 
  First theory said that birds can know where they are by watching the Sun and the stars. However, the professor pointed out that the Sun and the stars cannot be always visible.
  In second theory, it is said that birds can go to and back from their destinations by memorizing landmarks. But the professor denied this theory by showing an exception that a bird which was taken to new area could come back to the destination. This means that there is no relation between birds' navigation system and the birds' ability of memorizing landmarks.
  Third theory indicated that the crystals involved in birds' beaks play an important role as a magnetized needle of a compass. However, in his lecture, the professor pointed out that the crystals is too small to work as a needle of a compass. He added that it must be more bigger to be sensible to the Earth's magnetic field.
