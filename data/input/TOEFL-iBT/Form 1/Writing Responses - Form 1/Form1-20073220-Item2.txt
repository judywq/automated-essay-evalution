I do agree that it is more important to choose to study subjects that you are interested in rather than subjects that will prepare you for a successful job or career.
Even if a certain subject would not be a successful or stable career path (such as performing arts), ultimately, the most important factor is that you are happy with what you do. It is inevitable that the world puts an overemphasis on material wealth which would be consequent of a successful career or job, but I personally would choose to be happy studying a subject that I truly enjoy than have to study something that I do not enjoy as much, just for the sake of being 'stable'.
Also, I believe you are prone to study harder and more enthusiastically for a subject which you truly enjoy and have an interest in, and therefore be successful in that subject while in school, whereas you may get an uncharacteristic grade on subjects that you do not truly enjoy but would help you in the long-run to survive in the material world.
My best friend, for example, has had a passion for the performing arts since the Third Grade, and she is planning to attend a college for performing arts to pursuit her dreams. Although being an actress or a producer is a very unstable job, it is something that my best friend truly enjoys doing. She says that when she is acting, although it is stressful at times, it makes her forget all the troubles she faces with her other subjects in school, and in a way it works as a stress-reliever for her.
Although it is not a very wise idea to completely disregard the material world and live a very poor life, ultimately, we only live our lives once, and it is best to live every day to it's fullest, enjoying every minute of it by doing or studying the things that you truly enjoy. The key to life is to seize the day, or "carpe diem"
