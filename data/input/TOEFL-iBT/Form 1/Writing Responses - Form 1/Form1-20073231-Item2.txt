I think that it is more important to prepare for a job than to study subjects you are interested in.
Firstly, studying is in most countries really expensive and you cannot study subjects you like without thinking that it costs money to your parents or to you. Thus, studying should not be a waste of time and money. Universities are supposed to prepare yourself for your career and not just to have fun. It would be useless to graduate in a major that wouldn't offer you any job. For example, I know some students who studied more than five years anthropology before being confronted to the fact that there are only two jobs a year offered to this students to teach this subject in my country, and there are hundreds of students who graduate in it every year. Universities in my country should not allow students to follow that kind of cursus.
Secondly, an important thing about studying is motivation. When you have a goal to reach, it is far more easier to study because it is real, you know where you are going and where you will end up. This motivation makes you even more focused and your grades will go up inevitably. You will become more excited about it, you will look for some internships to get an insight on your future career. If your parents still help you out financially for your studies, you will look forward to being independant and earning your own salary. If you already work to pay your studies, you will soon stop doing a low-paid job in a fast food or a mall. 
In conclusion, preparing for a new job is really exciting, you see a bright future coming nearer,  you get excited about new experiences and you will meet other people and discover new countries and new cities, instead of remaining in a class to keep on studying as you have already done for more than 15 years.
