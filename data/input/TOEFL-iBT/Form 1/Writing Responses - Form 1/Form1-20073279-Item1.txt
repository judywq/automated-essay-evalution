In his lecture, the professor exposes the weaknesses of the three theories about how migrating birds navigate. 
First of all, he criticizes the theory that birds orient their flight toward either the sun or the North Star by pointing out that neither the stars nor the sun are always visible, whereas birds are able to find their way even in cloudy and foggy weather conditions.
Secondly, he emphasizes that birds will even be able to navigate their way to their destination when they are released in a completely unfamiliar environment, thereby contradicting the theory and migrating birds navigate by remembering landmarks such as rivers or mountains.
Finally, as exciting as a magnetic beak might sound when first hearing about it, an internal compass, or simply knowing you are headed south, does not serve any purpose without the bird knowing where it is currently located and how far its location is from its destination.
However, the professor also states that each of the three theories has its support and that it is likely that, all theories put together, the phenomenon of migrating birds can be explained.
