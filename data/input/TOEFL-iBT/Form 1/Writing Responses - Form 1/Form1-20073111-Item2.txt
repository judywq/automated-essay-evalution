I do disagree with this statement because in life and later on in a job - career there are many different things we have to deal with. 
 Life and career is not only about the things we are interested in or we like.
It will also bring up things to us we are not so interested in.
Very often during career subjects show up we have learned in school where we thought we would never need them but later on we realise that we actually  do need them.
For example a medical doctor might not be interested in book-keeping and numbers but once he runs a practice and has employees he might have to deal with things like that.
A technical engineer might not be interested in correct spelling or writing but later on he would also have to write letters to his clients and it might not give a good impression to his costumers when he spells words in the wrong way.
 Do me that happened with mathematics and geometrics. I thought - what for would I ever need this ? Now that I have a medical job I realise that I need mathematics quite a lot. 
 I do also think that children very often can not decide what is important and what is not - so if students would only choose subjects they are interested in they could miss a lot of important information they will need later on in life. 
Also learning about new things very often shows us new things we do actually like. How could I know if I like something if I have never heard about it or tried it before.
In summary I think the more we learn about new things and the more we try - the better it will be for the  future and the greater gets our understanding of the world and  less problems will occure  later on.
