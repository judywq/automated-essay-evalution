I have discussed the topic in scope many times with my friends during the last years. Some years ago, we all made our big choices and selected universities and now some are already working when others are soon graduating. It is interesting to discuss these choises, as we now can see how our future careers might become. 

Choosing between study subjects of interest or the one preparing for a career is not easy. What makes it even more difficult is that these decisions have to be made by young people with little work experience. These decisions that will affect their whole life. Will a university degree in economics or technology be the right choice even though a young person could have the skills to work with e.g. art.

I believe people need to choose subjects they are interested in, but every person should consider whether their interests and skills are of such a level, that they can also bring money to their homes and support themselves by these study subjects. Living a life doing something just for the money will give little meaning to each day. All persons do not, however, have specific interests and they can find it difficult to choose, as they do not even know what they could do or what they want to do.

It is a big choice to make and it will affect your future years. I have friends who became economists, but started talking about studying literature the day they graduated. They are still in the economic fieldm but I would not be so sure about that in ten years from now.

Working with something for many years requires interest in the subject and money does not talk if the person is not found of what he or she is doing. I know many older people with one exam who have left a career or a top job in order to concentrate on something more practical, usually a profession with less stress involved. Luckily having one exam does not mean there cannot be another one.
