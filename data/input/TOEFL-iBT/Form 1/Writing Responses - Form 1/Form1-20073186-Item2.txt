Today we have to work hard to get a good job. But is it our decision in what academic field we do our best?
As I started studying biochemistry, I was doing what I wanted to do. You may think I support doing what you are interested in. But I'm against that. In the first two years I had to do a lot of physical chemistry and at school I never thought  I would do physics anymore. But I learned it too, of course. My argument for doing things that you are maybe not interested in, is that you sometimes not know what is important for your aims. And for my aim, doing biochemistry, it is important to understand physical basics. 
I don't know what my future job may be. And that is another convincing argument for preparing for future life. You cannot only prepare by doing interesting things like art, music and other things many people want to do because you don't know what your future is about. Perhaps instead of doing experiments in biochemistry or genetics I will have to explain students or workers basic physics.
Moreover in my opinion you could become very frustrated by doing something you like to do. Think about that: You are writing books and nobody wants to read them. On the one hand, you will have no money for life in good conditions and on the other you perhaps are so frustrated that nobody wants to talk to you. Based on my experience not everone is qualified enough in every subject.
In addition I think it is more important that you do what you want in your free time. Then you have two worlds: Your job where you must do something and your hobbies where you can do what you like. If one part of your life, maybe your job, is bad for a distinct time, you still have the second world.
Many students I know started to study something they were interested in. They stopped it because they saw that their special subject isn't something for their whole life.
It is more important to be prepared for your job instead of doing what you are interested in.
