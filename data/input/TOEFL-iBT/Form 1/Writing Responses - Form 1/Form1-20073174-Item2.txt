Although many people may argue that a man should study only things that may really help him in his further career, I believe that it is very important that one studies subjects that his is interested in. My view may be supported by the following arguments:

First of all, the most important thing that people usually try to achieve through the development of their career is a self-satisfaction. However, I think that it is more probable to reach such a self-satisfaction through involving in the things you are interested in. So, a person is unlikely to achieve a self-satisfaction in studying of law if it always dreamed about mathematics or economics.

Further, no one can guess in advance what subjects will be actually helpfull in his future life. There are a lot of examples in my life when people started a career which they previously did not ever dreamed of. So, for example, one of my further's collegues turned from an engeneer into a farmer, and one of my classmates who studied law once became a landscape architecture.

Finally, it may be more easy to a person to study the subjests of his interest. If one studies something that he actually does not want to study, it perpetually suffers from the nessecity to supress his real interests. Simulateneously, peple who enjoy the subjects they elected are usually more enthusiastic in their studying and, as a consequence, are more successfull.

In light of the above, it is more reasonable to study the subjects you really interested in, because you may benefit from such a choice from acheivement of self-satisfaction and success.
