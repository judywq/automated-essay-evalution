In the reading passage three theories which have been proposed to explain birds migrations are presented, while in the lecture this information is completed  explaining the main objections against each theory.
    The first theory suggests that birds are driven by Sun and stars while migrating. In the lecture the professor underlines that this theory does not take into account the fact that both Sun and stars can be obscured by clounds, and birds are able to migrate during cloudy days as well.
   The second theory instead, is based on some studies showing that birds whose hippocampal region is damaged, show serious deficencies when trying to perform spatial ability and memory tasks. This might imply that this area is fundamental for migrating, since birds probablty remember landmarks to orient themselfs. But this theory cannot fully explain birds migrations as well, since it has been shown that birds placed in an unknown area are able to find their way anyway.
    Finally, the third theory, tries to explain birds migrations through their ability to use magnetite which  is embedded in their beaks. In fact, this mineral, combined with the Earth's magnetic field, could be used as an internal compass. Obviously, as underlinded during the lecture, this "compass" does not provide information about position, and hence it is not able, by itself, to provide information about the route to follow.
   The main point that can be inferred from the lecture and the reading passage is that only a theory obtained combaining these three other theories is able to fully explain birds migration.
