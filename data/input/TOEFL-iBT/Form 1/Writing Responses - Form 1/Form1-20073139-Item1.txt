	The birds migrate long distances and navigate accurately so the puzzled scientist have put forth theories with only some facts to back it up.
	One of the theories suggests that the birds migrate with the help of the sun's path. The sun's east to west movement gives them the sense of direction. The birds that migrate by night use the stars or specifically the north star to take them north. But this theory has some flaws as the sun and the stars are not visible all the time.
	Second suggests that the birds migrate with the help of memorization of the landmarks. This is done by the hippocampal region of the brain. Although, there have been tests which show that the birds migrate from the unknown regions.
	Third theory suggests a magnetic crystal formation in the beak of the birds. Which hels them point to the north. They use this magnet which responds to the earth's magnetic pole showing them the north and south.
	All the theories have some kind of flaw or the other as just knowing the directions is not enough but also knowing how far the destination resides. Although the above theories explain how the birds get the sense of direction to be able to know the exact place in that direction is another thing.
