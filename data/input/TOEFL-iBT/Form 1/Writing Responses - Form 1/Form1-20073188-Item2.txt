Although different people might have different oppinions on this topic, for me it's definitely more important to choose to study subjects I'm interested in than choosing subjects to prepare for a good job or career.

It's maybe some kind of unfair to ask me about this topic, since I'm studying computer science. I study this, because I'm highly interested in these subjects and I realy enjoy doing it. Anyway, it also automaticaly prepares me for a good position or career in the IT industry - even if that is not my main goal.

But let me explain why in my oppinion it's much more important to choose your subjects depending on your interest.

First, if your're interested in the things you do, you probably also enjoy doing these things. If you enjoy these things, learning, doing project work and passing test is much easier for you because you don't have to force yourself all the time to get things done. You probably have to do this frequently if you choose a career orientated subject, you're maybe not very interested in. This makes your time at university maybe a successfull time but you have to pay the price. 

Second, think of your future! If you aren't interested in your subjects at university, you most likely won't enjoy them later in your job. Think about, that you probably will spent at least two-third of your live on your job. You probably become successfull on your job, but maybe not very happy. For me the essence of live is being happy, so choosing subjects only focused on your carrer can possibly lead to a - in my oppinion - wasted life. And I don't think that money is a replacement for happienes. 

Well, these two points reflect only my very own oppinion on this topic. But - at least for me - it works!
