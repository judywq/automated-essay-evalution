The lecture discusses three theories about the ability of migrating birds to acurately navegate long jouneys.
A profesor discussed the limitations of these theories, he does not say that they are not acurate, but he expresses his opinion on why they do not clarify by themself the puzzle naturalists face when studing migrating birds' ability to navigate.
The first theory states that the birds use celestial objects as a reference. The profesor stated that it cannot be the sole source of refernce, since celestial objects like the sun and the stars are not always available, for example on a cloudy day.
The second theory claims that the migrating birds use landmarks as navegational reference. The profesor expresses that this would mean that a bird could not find its way back from  a location it is not familiar with. Birds do not only relay on their memory of landmarks to navigate.
The third theory states that the birds use a type of internal compass to respond to Earth's magnetic field. the profesor states that the bird needs to know where it is and where it's heading in order to navigate, an internal compass would not be enough.
