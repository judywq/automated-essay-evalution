The lecture doesn't give too much credit to the three theories of bird migration presented in the passage. The professor states that the three reasons mentioned in the passage are only partially true and don't completely explain the process of bird migration.

The first reason suggested in the passage is that the birds use celestial objects to orient themselves directionally in the process of migration. The passage uses the east/west orientation with respect to the Sun's path and the direction utilizing North Star to support the usage of celestial objects by the birds. However, the lecture doesn't completely agree with this reason. According to the professor these celestial objects are not visible all the time, especially when covered by clouds. However, there has been evidence that birds are still able to migrate even in such cases when the celestial objects are visible.

The second reason stated in the passage is that bird use landmarks such as rivers, mountains, lakes etc to form a memory image of the regular paths and use these memory images in the process of migration. However, as per the lecture this reason is not completely justifiable as there have been studies where birds have been left in completely unknown territories and still they have managed to fly back to their nest. This refutes the landmark memory theory.

The third reason stated in the passage is the usage of birds internal compass system to align themselves during the flight based on the earth's magentic field. The lecture is not in agreement with this theory either. As per the professor just knowing knowing the direction of travel is not good enough, what is more important is to know the distance from the origin/destination. This information is not given by the compass and doesn't explain how the birds know when to stop.
