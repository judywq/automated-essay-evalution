   There is an old saying that "a genious cannot bit a someone who makes great efforts on it, and someone who makes great efforts cannot bit a someone who enjoy it." To take pleasure from something would be the strongest motivation to do something. 
   Most of conflicts between parents and their sons or daughters in teenage come from the problem that parents do not permit their kids to do what they really want to. Of course, if it were a criminal that kids want to do, then parents should prevent them from committing it. However, in most cases, parent usually force their kids to do what parents want to, not their kids want to. 
   Kids do not have any incentives to do what parents said unless they like to do it. I think it is totally required for parents to give their kids opportunities to explore what they are really care about and what they want to do for their future.  
   To choose study subject is on the same context. If a student does not enjoy what they study, they cannot have any motivation to do it, consequently, they cannot accomplish it successfully. Pleasures can be the strongest motivation to make themselves strive to complete it. 
   It is inevitable for anyone to face great difficulties while they study. However, if someone has sincere enthusiasm what he or she is doing, he or she is willing to overcome those difficulties and ultimately, can improve himself or herself in their own field.  
   In addition, on the process of doing their best, it is pretty sure that they can figure out the proper job they can do for their lives. To prepare for a job itself cannot be the sole purpose. It can be just an outcome of thier doing best with pleasure. Hence, I do not agree on the statement in the question and I think it is more important to choose to study subject he or she is interested in.
