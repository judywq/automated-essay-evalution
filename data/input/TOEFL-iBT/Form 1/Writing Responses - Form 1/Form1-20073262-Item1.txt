According the lecture, all these three explanations for bird's migration amout to partial-explanations

The first one , that is ,the theory of reference to celestial objects pales before the fact that when suns or stars are invisible or the weather is cloudy , birds can still manage to navigate in the right direction.

The second theory that birds naviogation is attributable to their memories of landmarks also is unconvicing. Because according to the presumption of this theory, the birds which are taken to a entirely new place would not be able to find their ways back to their nests. Unfortuately, the result of the test is just the opposite. New location has little effect on birds' navigation

The last explanation resulting from the theory of birds' internal compass may not be sufficient to prove every aspect of the migration. As we all know, navigation is an issue not only about direction but also about location and length. Thus, this theory lacks conviction when it comes to the fact that birds can accomplish their migration no matter where they are and how faraway they leave home.

In sum, all three theories of birds' navigation are not completely persuasive.
