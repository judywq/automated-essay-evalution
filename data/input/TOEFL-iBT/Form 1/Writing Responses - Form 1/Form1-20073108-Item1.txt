The lecture basically states the three theories that explain the abiliy of migrating birds to navigate long journeys.  The professor starts his lecture saying that all of the three theories have some type of support, meaning that all three of them may be true in some point.  The truth is, each of the theories make partial explanations on how birds migrate from one point to another, reaching their destinations at all times.

The first theory which suggests that migrating birds fly in reference to celestial objets is not completely true, or in other words, does not support completely the abilities of the birds to migrate from one point to another, because these celestial objets, such as the sun and the stars, are not always visible during day or night time.  Therefore, if clouds are on their way for example, the birds wouldn't be able to locate a point on the sky and follow it through their journey.  

The second theory states that birds use landmarks to navigate while they are migrating.  This one  is not said to be the only one because if we put a bird in a place where it has never been before, the bird will not have the ability to recognize its surroundings and therefore it will be unable to go back to its habitat.  

The final point made in the lecture was regarding the third theory, which states that the birds might have an internal compass that responds to the earth's magnetic field.  The professor concludes that it is possible for birds to guide their ways using the crystals in their beaks, but that it is not possible to state that it is the only way for them to guide themselves.
