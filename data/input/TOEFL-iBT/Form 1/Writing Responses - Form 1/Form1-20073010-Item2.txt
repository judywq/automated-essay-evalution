	Sometimes people study things that never interested them, they just took they needed it and not because they wanted it. This is the case of a lot of students in college who take classes that they do not like, but it will help them in their career a lot; so they made a sacrifice for a better future. But in my opinion that is not the best thing to do. 
	For example, my cousin in college had 2 choices between taking one class that he knew it was going to help him a lot with his career, but he just did not like the class; and on the other side he had a class that he loved and he always wanted to take it, even though he knew it would not help him as much as the other course, he ended up doing what he loved, not what he needed it.
	It was a very hard decision to make for him, but the reason he chose that is because he knew he was going to enjoy it more, and learn way more things from it, that may not help him a lot in his career; so he putted a lot of effort on the class he loved, and I am very sure that he learned more and got many great experiences from it. Instead if he would have taken the first option, he was just going to pass the class, and not care about what he learned, because it would have been just one more class for him, and he could have regreated forever, because the class never interested him, he just took it, because he needed it.
	So, I think is more important to study  something that you are interested with than to study something that you need for a job or career, because that class that you love might not help you a lot with your job and career, but it sure will help you a lot in life and that is what really matters for me.
