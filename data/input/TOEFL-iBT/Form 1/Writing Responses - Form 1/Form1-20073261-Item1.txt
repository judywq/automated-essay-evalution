   There are three theories for explaining the ablility of migrating birds. However, each theory does not perfectly explain all of the situations. 
   First, it seems to make a sense that birds can navigate in reference to celetial objects such as the Sun or the stars. However, the Sun and the stars are not always visible. Some studies showed that birds moved when there were no sunshine or the stars. 
   Second, another theory claims that birds can navigate using landmarks such as rivers, coastlines, or mountains. It also claims that birds memorize those kinds of landmarks and follow them. However, some experiments which tried to release birds in totally unknown place shows birds can navigate without any memories about landmarks. 
   Finally, another theory which says that birds have internal compass to respond to Earth's magnetic field is alos not enough to demonstrate all kinds of migrating birds. Some birds are found that they do not moved exactly same direction of the way of flow of Earth's magneric. Also birds cannot get their accurate destination only with responding to magnetic field. 
   In short, all theories are usefule but just give us only  partial explanations about the birds' migrating.
