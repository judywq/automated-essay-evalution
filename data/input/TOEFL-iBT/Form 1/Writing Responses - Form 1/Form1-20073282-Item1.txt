Every year birds undergo long journeys in which the migrate south. Scientists keep trying to explain how is it that these creatures can navigate without getting lost. They have developed three theories in order to explain this phenomenum. The first one relates their derection to celestial objects like the moon and the stars. However, according to the lecture this does not completeley satisfy how birds migrate because even if they could use the sun or the moon for direction, these are not reliable points of reference due to the fact that they are not always present. The second theory states that birds navigate through the use of landmarks such as rivers or mountains. This theory is also defiable, because even if they did guide themselves using these landmarks, their memories could fool them. If they were released in an environment away from where they inhabit, then they would not find their way back since the landmarks at this new location differ from the ones at the other location. The third theory relates birds to the magnetic field in the center of the earth. If in some way there was a form of magnatite incrusted in their beaks, then this would serve as a compass with which to direct themselves. However, direction is not sufficient to perform these lengthy journeys. Other factors like distance, speed and velocity have to be considered if the bird wants to reach its destination safely and effectively.
