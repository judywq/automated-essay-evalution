In the lecture professor explains the limitations of the proposed theories of  navigation of migrating birds.
The first theory suggests that birds navigate in reference to celestial objects such as sun and stars. Professor explains this is not applicable always, because Sun and other stars are not visible all the time. It might be in the clouds. Birds still can navigate at nights when Sun and other stars are not visible.
The second theory explains the birds navigate by landmark like rivers and mountains. Professor says if they are exposed to a new location, and they navigate by using landmarks birds won't be make it back to their nest. Because the land marks are not the same as what stored in their memory. Some scientists have trsted this by exposing birds to a new location and they still managed to get back to their nests. So birds are not only depending on the landmarks but also on something else to navigate.
The third theory proposes that birds have an internal compass in their beak which helps them to respond to earth's magnetic field and navigate. Professor explains that with the help of this magnetic compass they will be able to find out whether they are heading south, north,east or west. But doesn't help them to reach a specific destination.
