  I am now an undergraduate student, and all I do is think about how to choose what field I should get in. At times I think about the jobs that are mostly needed, but then I remind myself that a successful job does not depend on what a person studies.
  I believe that when a person is interested in something, he gives their best in it and try to work very hard to make it succeed. 
  Life is already hard as it is, and having to work hard on something you don't like would make it much harder. For example, a student who chooses to study business just because he would get a better job or career than if he studied arts, is going to be spending 4 years of his life studying something he does not like and he has to work hard on it to get the best results and then later on spend his life working on this field while he prefers working with art. And he's going to be giving a lot of effort to make his job succeed, but he is not enjoying this effort. There are lots of times when a person feels great and proud of himself when he works hard on something he likes and succeeds. But what if this person had to work really hard on something he did not enjoy just to make it succeed?
  I believe that it is up to each person to make his career successful or not. Ofcourse this does not mean that a person should study something that is impossible to work with just because he enjoys doing it. As long as the field he chooses is available in careers and the person enjoys doing it then he should go for it, but he should make sure to remember that even though its hard work, but it is hard work gone into something he likes.
