	In this set of materials, the reading passage clarifies three theories how birds migrate, and the listening lecture explains that no  one theory can solely explain birds' migration.
	
	The reading passage gives us information about the three theories about migration.  The first theory is that birds navigate by the celestial objects in the sky. The birds which migrate at noon, would fly relative to the Sun's path, and the birds which migrate at night would fly relative to the North Star. The second theory is that birds navigate by remembering the landmarks, using the hippocampal region of the brain. The third theory is that birds have an internal compass inside their bodies, called Magnetite that react to the magetic field of the Earth, and navigate using Magnetite.
	
	It is implied in the listening lecture, that these three methods  only partially help the birds to navigate. The first theory, that birds navigate relative to the celestial objects cannot perfectly explain the birds' migration in asmuch as these celestial objects, such as sun and stars, are not always visible due to the clouds. The second theory, memorizing the landmark also cannot explain the birds' migration solely. It can be proven by the analysis that birds can come back to their nest, even from places they are unfamiliar. The last theory cannot explain the birds' migration too. It is true that birds can figure out which ways they are going, north or south, using the internal compass, but it cannot explain how birds know which way they should go.
