In this lecture and text , we learn that birds have different ways of finding their nest or going around the world without getting lost.

The text gives us three theories to think about, the first one is the fact that birds may use celestial objects such as stars and planets to guide themselves.During the day they might use the sun and during the night they will use other stars (like the North star that directs them easily towards a northern direction). But this is limited with the possibility of bad weather and the impossibility for the birds to find the sun and stars because they might be hidden by clouds.

The second theory is the ability of the birds to remember landmarks and to actually find their way using rivers,mountains,etc to find the way to their nest. This theory can be attacked with the fact that scientist have tried releasing birds in a place that they had not visited yet they could find the way to their nest.

The final theory talks about the presence of Magnetite inside the bird's beak , the bird would have an internal compass that is made with the earth's magnetic field. This theory only works to an extent because you cannot find a special place by just using directions, birds need an idea of how far their nest is and where do they have to turn.
