Scientists ask how birds can plan their long journeys and they give us three theories how this great ability can be explained. Nevertheless no theory can convince us completely.
By the first theory we are told that birds use celestial objects to move in the right direction. So they could be dependent on the sun or stars like the North Star. But what is if there are clouds and the sun isn't visible? Therefore this theory can't be the whole thing.
The second one suggests that birds navigate by landmarks. As a result by this theory birds have to momorize special environmental things like mountains or rivers. But an experiment shows us that this theory also can't explain everything: If you bring a bird to a place that is unkown to it because it could have be never there, it can flight home. This really speaks against what was postulated.
You could believe in the last theory: Birds could have an internal compass that reacts on magnetic fields like the Earth's one. But that even explains not enough because a compass only shows you directions but no information about distance.
We see that science is very good in finding out new things. But not everything can be explained by our knowledge.
