	In both listening and reading passage we can find information about the ability of the migrating birds to navigate long journeys.
	In the reading passage there are stated three different theories about the birds' navigational abilities. The first theory supports the navigation according to celestial objects. The second theory introduce us to the ability of the birds navigating by landmarks and lastly the third theory supports the idea of birds' magnetic field use.
	The listening passage comes to present limitations of the three theories mentioned above by giving examples. As for the first theory the listening passage states that the sky is not always quite visible and clear but clouds remain on it. Even then, the birds are still in the position to navigate. In regard of the second theory it is stated that birds are unable to find the way home when start a journey. The place they want to go remains uknown to them, and professor comes to the conclusion that they do not rely only on their memory. Last but not least, the third theory presents also limitations because the magnetic field can also shows you from where you began and how far you are.
	To sum up, the reading passage analyzes three theories that concern the ability of migrating birds to navigate long journeys while the listening passage presents limitations of those theories.
