     In the lecture, it is said that the theories given to explain the ability of migrating birds to accurately navigate long distances has only supports and partial explanations. One cannot totally rely on these theories.
     For the first theory of birds navigating in reference to celestial bodies can be argued on a very simple observation. If birds depend on the sun, the moon and the stars then how do they travel to their destinations on cloudy days when they cannot see the sun, moon and the North Star.
      Furthermore, the birds even donot depend only on their memorizing power i.e. the hippocampal region of the brain. If a bird is carried to an unknown place, even then it can trace back to the place where it has come from.So, memorizing cannot play a part here as the place in unknown to the bird.
      Neither the third theory of internal compass is a proper explanation. In order to use this technique it is important to know where you are and how far the destination is which is not available to a bird. Hence, the theory of magnetite crystals serving as internal compasses donot not explain the phenomena of migration.
      All these theories only partially explain this unusual phenomenon of the migrating birds and puzzles the naturalists.
