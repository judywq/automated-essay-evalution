The three theories about how birds can travel long distance are not all correct.
For the first idea,the problem is the sun and the stars are not always being seen. Birds may follow the stars and the sun in a fine weather,but  they can still find directions when it is cloudy.It doesn't only depend on the direction of sun or polar star.
The idea of landmark is not reasonable enough. When the birds travel to somewhere they have never been,they will know no landmarks. And things may change in many situations such as a river may dry out, the forest may be burnt.
The third theory is more scientific, but it is still not all of it. Only the basic direction is not enough for the birds to find a certain place. It doesn't help to know the distance or what the destination look like. 
There's still things to discover, about how birds can navigate long journeys.
