As you say "there is no lunch for free" - actually I find it difficult to vote for one position ignoring the pros and cons on both sides. But even if it is not easy I would favorite the given statement that it is better to "fall in love" with you study subjects than to choose too rational, paralysed by economic needs. 
When I was young my father gave me the advice not to look on money but on life. And the meaning of "life" referres to the immaterial values itselfs: harmony, consciousness, joy, happiness. So you can be happy in helping other people, feel personal freedom when enjoying the sunrise or feeling successfully and proud on writing a poem without increasing your income. To "fall in love" with the subjects one studies assure you - nearly for sure - not to get depressed or uneffective in what you are doing. You will be more motivated and content when studying the fields you are interrested in. Due to this interest you work harder and with more success because you are ambitious and in the long run proud of what you create. To put it short: we are all good in what we love!
