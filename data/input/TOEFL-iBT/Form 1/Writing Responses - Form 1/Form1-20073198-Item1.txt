	None of the three theories presented can completely explain the facts observed in nature as regards the migration of birds.
	Are birds using the stars or the sun as a map? Birds can still find their way when the sky is cloudy, so the reference to celestial objects to explain this phenomenon is, at least, imperfect. This does not mean that birds can use these objects to orient themsleves, but the explanation as a whole is insufficient.
	The second theory claims that birds use their memory to migrate. Although they could use landmarks such as rivers as a reference, some studies contradict this theory. In these studies, birds have been released far away from familiar places and they could still find their way home. 
	Finally, a theory attributes the ability of migrating birds to a built-in compass. This explanation seems incomplete for the following reason: knowing that you are heading south does not give you information of where exactly you are going or where you departed from.
	Most likely a combination of the three theories is the right answer. Birds can have multiple methods of orientation that do not necessarily conflict with each other.
