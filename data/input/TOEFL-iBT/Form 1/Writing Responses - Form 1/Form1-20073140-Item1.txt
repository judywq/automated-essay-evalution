In this set of material the reading passage describes three theories of birds navigating their way on extremely long journeys and the listening passage gives examples why these three theories are limited.

The first theory in the reading passage suggests that birds are able to navigate their way by using the sun, at day time, and the stars, exspecially the North Star, at night.
The listening passage mentiones that this theory is limited because birds are not able to see the sun or stars always because they are not always visible.

The second theory in the reading passage claims that birds are able to navigate by using lanmarks, such as rivers, coastlines and mountains.
The listening passage says that this theory is also limited because that would mean, that when a bird would be taken to a place he has never been to before, he would not be able to find his way back to his nest. But studies did show, that a bird is able to navigate and find his way back to his nest.

The third theory in the reading passage says that birds have an internal compass with wich they are able to respond to magnetite Earth fields to navigate.
The listening passage claims that this theory is also limited because a internal compass would not be enough for a bird to navigat and that  birds know where they are and how far it is away to their nest.
