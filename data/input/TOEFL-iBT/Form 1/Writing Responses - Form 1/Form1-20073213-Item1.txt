The lecture discusses different theories on bird`s navigational skills. The professor intends to make it clear to the audience that neither of the three theories he mentioned, considered alone, can give a complete account on the topic.  

The firts theory states that birds use celestial bodies to navigate. According to this theory, the sun and the stars are used by birds to locate themselves and to follow directions to their destinations. However, as the professor explains, celestial bodies are not always visible, due to wheather conditions. Since birds can still navigate on such conditions, the theory can not fully explain bird`s navigational skills.

Another theory described defends that birds rely on landmarks to navigate, memorizing the landscape and following that mental blueprint to navigate. Altough there is data to support the theory, the professor points out that it fails to explain how birds can locate themselves when they are on unknow territorry.

Finnaly, the third theory suggests that birds have a natural compass in their beaks, which is filled with a substance that alows birds to navigate on earth`s magnetic fields. The professor points out that a compass would not be enough to navigate, since birds would still need a mechanism to assess the position of the origin and destination and the distance between them.
