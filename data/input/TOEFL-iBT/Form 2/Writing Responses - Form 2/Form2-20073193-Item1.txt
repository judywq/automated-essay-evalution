The man givig the lecture first states that all of the points made in the passage can be challenged, they depend on your point of view. 
First of all, the lecturer notes that the local fish the author of the article mentions are already endangered and that this is not caused by the farms but rather to prior overfishing by the traditional fishing industry. So the wild fish to protect are a minor concern, as they themselves are a minority in these habitats. Therefor the spreading of diseases is a not a immediate danger.
Then he goes on and talks about the chemical substances used to enhance the fishes' mass. In his opinion, people should not compare these chemically fed fish to wild life fish, but rather to the other meat that enters our production chain, like pork, cattle and so on. These are fed growth inducing substances on regular basis with no major health consequences so far. He even explains that fish meat is less fatty than other meat and therefor provides a healthy and nutritional food source.
Finally he claims that the so-called wastefullness the author writes about is in fact helping to increase the number and mass of edible fish, as the protein concentrates are made from fish that would otherwise only be the byproduct of fishing or that would not enter the food chain at all.
