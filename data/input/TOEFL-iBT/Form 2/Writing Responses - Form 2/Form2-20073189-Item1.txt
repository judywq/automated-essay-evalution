In the reading passage it is said that the infections developed in farm-raised fish are dangerous to the wild fish living nearby and thus endanger the local populations. The lecturer stated that commercial fishing has reduced the number of wild fish in the fish farming areas. Farming fish for commercial use allows the wild fish populations to rebound, which, according to the lecturer, is much more important than the possible infections. 
Another problem mentioned in the text is the possible health risk to humans. According to the lecturer, the chemicals used on farmed fish are the same ones given to poultry, beef and pork, which people already consume in large quantities. Fish contains less fat and has a better nutritional value than other farmed meat, so fish is, in fact, better for human. 
It is also stated in the reading passage, that fish farming is wasteful. The lecturer, however, says that even though some species of farmed fish are fed with fish meal, this meal is usually made of fish that are usually not eaten by human. So the fish farmers are actually turning ineatable fish into eatable fish.
