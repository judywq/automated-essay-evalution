The professor begins his argument by saying that fish farming is often pictured as being a not so good method of ensuring fish. He then starts looking at the arguments that are often invoked by the critics of this procedure.

According to the professor, many species of fish are damaged because of over fishing, whereas the text argues that farming a great number of fishes in a small area can cause fish diseases, that can subsequently spread to wild fishes. In his opinion, overfishing is thus prevented from happening. 

The second point that the professor makes regards the chemicals that are used to help farm raised fishes get bigger. In his opinion, fish meat should be regarded just like the rest of the meat people eat, for example poultry or beef. The same principle is applied for feeding them and therefore there is no difference. He also considers that actually fish meat is healthier than others, simply because fish meat is less fat. 

The last point made is related to the argument raised in the reading passage, about wasting fish meat to feed the farm raised fish. He admits to such a practice and the argument he uses is that the meat fed to the fishes is mostly made out of species of fish that humans do not eat. According to him, this process transforms inedible fish into edible fish.
