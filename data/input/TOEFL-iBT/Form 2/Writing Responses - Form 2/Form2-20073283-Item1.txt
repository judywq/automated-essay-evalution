The topic of the passage is about bad consequences caused by fish farming. The passage then gives some reasons why fish farming is a threat to the environment. However, the lecturer says that the danger caused by fish farming is not as bad as described in the passage.

First, the passage says that fish farming jeopardizes the health of the wild fish. According to the lecturer, the population of wild fish nowadays has already reach a worrying level. He goes on to say that this is mainly caused by commercial fishing, and not by fish farming activities. As a matter of fact, he says that the increase of fish farming can decrease commercial fishing, which in the end can help to increase the population of wild fish.

The second reason given in the passage is that farm-raised fish may have health issues. The passage says that growth inducing chemicals used to feed the fishes may be harmful for humans. The lecturer, however, mentions that the same chemicals are also used by pig and cow farmers. Fish, having more nutritions and less fat compared to beef or pork, then would still be a healthier choice to eat.

Lastly, the passage says that the third consequence of fish farming is the wastefulness of the process. It goes on to say that farm-raised fish will reduce the number of fishes from the sea. The lecturer counters that by saying that the fish meal that is used to feed fish in fish farm, is actually fishes that can not be eaten by humans. It means that it make use of inedible fishes that were supposed to be thrown away.

In conclusion, the lecturer says fish farming is not as harmful as the passage describe. Fish farming even has some advantages for the enviroment.
