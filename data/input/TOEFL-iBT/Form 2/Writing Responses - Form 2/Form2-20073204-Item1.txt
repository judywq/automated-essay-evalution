The lecture opposes the points made in the text about the fish farming and its effects on the wild fish. 
First, the the narrator disagrees that fish farming really jeopardizes the health of wild fish in the area around the farm. He notices that the wild fish is endangered already and states that the positive effect produced by the fish farming is much more important than the negative one.
Second, the lecturer points out that although the fact that the chemicals used in the fish farming to produce bigger fish faster could be harmful for the humans is true; the same chemicals are used to produce pork or beef and that meat is a far less healthy nutrition for peaple.
Third, the fact that the fish on the farms are often fed with a food made by processing wild fish is not a negative factor at all because this wild fish can not be eaten by people anyhow, so by feeding it to the farm fish we actually transform noneatable fish into an eatable fish.
