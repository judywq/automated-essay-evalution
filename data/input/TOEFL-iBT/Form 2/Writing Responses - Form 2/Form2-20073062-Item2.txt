     In general, I think that the significance of cooperation among people has not been changed much, even in modern society. It is because cooperation in the past was also quite important, and we need not to cooperate with other people not at all time.
     First, there were also much group-workings in the past. For example, the building of ancient empires like pyramids or temples required the teamwork. There must have been a lot of labors, and the process would be divided into organizational tasks. It means that the workers should cooperate well to properly finish the construction. It could be compared with group work in the company. Also, in Chosun dynasty in the Middle Age of the Korea, there were gruop agricultural task among the farmers. They supported farming of the other people, and next time, they were aided by people they had helped. Thus, in Chosun society, person who did not cooperate with the others should do all of the work on their own. These examples show that the cooperation was as important as that of today.
     The other reason is that nowadays, there are many jobs or tasks which can be done by singular person. People increasingly work in their home with the Internet today. It means, individual work is becoming important part of the job. Also, as the individualism has spreaded into the global world, people value on 'me' rather than 'we'. It means that in today, people prefer their own ability than the ability to company with other people well.
     For these reasons, I maintain the assertion that the importance of cooperating in nowaday's society is approximately same with that of the world in the past.
