      Since the 1960s, fish farming has become an increasingly common method of commercial fish production. But, unfortunately, there are some problems.
      First, a large number of fishes are in a small area for fish farming, but some diseases could be developed in the surroundings and that can easily spread to wild fish. Farmars are trying to prevent with medicines, but it's not so easy to accomplish.
      Second, the chemicals that used by farmers possibly make some influences to the humans who eat the fishes. In order to produce the bigger fish, there are plenty of medicines and chemicals are used, so that the risks for the health also could be increasing.
      Third, the fish farm uses too much edible fishes for the fishmeal. That means they discard the proteins available from the sea. Therefore, that is no longer than a wastefulness of fish products.
