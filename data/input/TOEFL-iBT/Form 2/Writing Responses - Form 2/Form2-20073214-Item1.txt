The lecture makes several very convincing points in order to prove the article wrong. On the one hand, fish farming doesn't endanger wild fish, which the article opposes to, because these types of fish have already been endangered before fish farming began in the 1960s. In fact, it is an alternative for wild fishing and can therefor be seen as an important factor for helping the wild fish regain a normal population size. Also, the chemicals used in the fish farms which are heavily criticized by the article are also used in poultry and beef. In addition, fish is in general healthier for the human body because it is low in fat and is therefor a good alternative despite the chemicals. The article also claims that fish farming isn't a very efficient method of breeding fish because in order to feed the fish, many more tons of wild fish have to die than are produced in the farm. The professor however argues that mainly wild fish which aren't eaten by humans are used for producing fish meal.
