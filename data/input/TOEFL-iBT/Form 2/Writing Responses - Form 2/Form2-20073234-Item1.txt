According to the lecture, fish farming is not as harmful to the wild fish as is traditional commercial fishing. More specifically, the dimishing use of traditional fishing lets the wild fish population grow back to acceptable levels. However, the lecturer makes no reference to the possible dissemination of infections or diseases from the farm-raised fish to the wild fish population.
The text poses the question of whether humans are "exposed to harmful or unnatural long-term effects" when consuming farm-raised fish, which are fed with growth-inducing chemicals. The lecturer questions the value of that concern as he recalls the examples other foods such as poultry that are also processed with artificial chemicals. Moreover, such foods are in general less healthy (due to amount of fat, etc.) than fish, he argues.
Lastly, the lecturer believes that using "several pounds of fish meal" to produce "one pound of farmed fish" is not a subject for concern. His reason is that the fish meal used in fish farming is not edible by humans and so its waste is acceptable.
