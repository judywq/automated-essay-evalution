 I totally agree that the ability to cooperate well with others is more important now than in the past due to the evolution of technology. The work has been more difficult and even impossible for individual workers, and the communication technology and transportation technology  broaden the size of work groups nowadays. Thus, we have to work with more people than before to accomplish more complex work, so the ability to cooperate with others well affects the effeciency and the result of our works.
 For example, medicians used to be just staying in their pharmacy and selling their medicines in the past. However, in today's world, they need to negotiate with the medical factories and cooperate with doctors. If they cooperate well, they will provide us the medicine which cures our illness more quickly in a reasonable price. If they don't cooperate well, there will be more complaints from the consumers and they will earn less money after all.
 About personal experience, I took some classes that ask a paper made in groups. And when I cooperated with other members well, everyone were more willing and involved in the work, and the grade would be higher. In contrast, when we didn't work along others well, the result would turn out terrible.
