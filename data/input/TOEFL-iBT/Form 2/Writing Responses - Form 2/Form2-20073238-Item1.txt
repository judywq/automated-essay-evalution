The professor tried to counter the notion that comercial fish farming has several  harmful consequences.
First of all, he does not agree that the farming harms the wild fish population. He mentioned that the wild fish population has already been harmed by commercial fishing. The wild fish diminishes in number because of over-fishing for human consumption. As the wid fish population has already been endangered by over-fishing, it is unfair to blame it on fish farming.
Secondly, it is not appropriate to put the blame on the use of chemicals in fish farming only. He did admit that fish farmers do use chemicals to increase harvest within a short time. Yet the point is that such practices are also common among poultry, pig and cow famers. To blame the fish farmer only is clearly unfair. Also, the nutricient value is much higher thatn that of pork or beef. Therefore, fish farming should be praised for producing healthy protein alternatives for human and improving their health instead of being critized for harming the health of human.
 Last but not least, fish farming should be credited for changing inedible fish into edible fish. Some accused the use of fish as fish-meal in  fish farming as a kind of wastage. It may be unfair because not all fish are suitable for human consumption. By feeding fish suitable for eating with fish which are not suitable for eating, the farming industry should not be accused of being wasteful.
With the above justification, the professor stated that we should not point an accusing finger on fish farming.
