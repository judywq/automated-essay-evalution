The lecturer criticizes the points made in the reading about how fish farm jeopardizes health of wild fish, causes health risks to human consumers, and is wasteful by providing several examples.

Firstly, the reading suggests that fish farm is a source of disease and parasitic infections among the wild fish populataton. The lecturer, however, criticizes that the wild fish population, which, according to the reading, is being threatened by the fish farm, were already endangered even before the fish farms were formed. He states that growing fish in the farm rather increases the population of wild fish by providing an alternative to fishing.

Secondly, the lecturer challenges the reading by saying that the chemicals used in fish farms are also used to produce large amount of beef and pork. He also says that fish has less fat and more nutritient than other meats, thereby attributing to the better health of human comsumers.

Lastly, the lecturer states that the fish meals used by fish farms are made up of fish that are inedible by humans; he says that the fish farms rather increases the amount of edible fish for humans, by using up inedible fish.

In conclusion, by making points that fish farm benefits the local wild fish population, the health of human consumers, and the amount of edible fish, the lecturer challenges the negative view of the reading.
