Some people may think that the we need to cooperate with others more today  than we used to in the past. However, I personally disagree with the statement that the ability to cooperate well with others is far more important than it was in the past for the following reasons.

First of all, technology today helps people a lot. We do not need much cooperation from others. For example, in my work place, I can do almost everything with computers and do not need to talk directly to anybody or ask for cooperation. When I want to take a day-off, I can type the date I will not come to the office on the computer system. I do not write it on a piece of paper and give it to someone to get apporoval. It is approved automatically. Moreover, when I ask for a reimburse of my bussiness trip fees, I fill an application form on the intranet. Later on, the computer system will get it processes. So, I can work without other people's cooperation. As the above example demonstrates, technology helps people more than other people do today.

Second, people today are financially independent today. Peope in the past were poor and they needed to cooperate with each other to survive. This is not the case today. For instance, about forty years ago, Japan where I live now was poorer and people in the neighbourhood helped each other. When they were temporarily lacking in spice or food, they used to ask their neighbours to give some food. And they return food when others were in trouble. However, today in Japan, this never happens. We have enough money to live our own lives and  plenty of food to eat. We do not need to borrow food or spice from our neighbours. As this experience clearly shows, people have much more money today than people did in the past.

In conclusion, I definitely believe that we need the ability to cooperate well with others more in the past than we do today.
