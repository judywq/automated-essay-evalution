In this set of materials both the reading and the listening passages deal with the subject of fish farming. In the listening passage, however, the speaker disagrees with the harmful effects of fish farming, pointed out in the text, and explains the reasons of holding this kind of opinion. 

According to the reading passage when large numbers of fish are kept closed on the relatively small area of the farm, there is a tendency of development of different kinds of diseases which can easily be caught by wild fish outside the farm. However, the speaker says that wild fish is more endangered of overfishing, than of illnesses conveyed by farmed fish. 

On the other hand, the lecturerer agrees that chemicals are used when raising farmed fish. Nevertheless, all stock-breeding branches use chemicals and fish meat is far more healthier than the other kinds of meat like pork, beef or poultry.

The third point, the speaker opposes, is the wastefulness of the process of fish farming in the long term. He says that the fish used to feed farmed fish is actually inetable and in fact fish farming contributes to the increase of etable fish.
