The lecture brings objections to each of the drawbacks described in the text against fish farming by giving another point of view in the explanation of each of the considered arguments.

First, he explains that fish farming of a particular species often occurs in areas where the corresponding wild species were already endangered because of extensive traditional farming. According to him, fish farming then fosters development of fish in the area because there is not so much need for fishing wild species any more. This challenges the text argument about harvested fish bringing disease to nearby wild species, thus endangering them.

Second, the lecturer disagrees with the fact that products from fish farms may endanger humans, as well as fish, because of the chemicals used in order to optimize fish production. According to him, this drawback is not specific to fish, and one would better balance the nutrients brought by fish with the ones brought by all other kinds of foods, especially meat.

Finally, he states that mainly (if not only) non eatable fish is used as food for farm-raised fish. This stem undermines the text assertion that farm fishing wastes wild fish for feeding raised ones. Therefore he asserts that fish farming increases the proportion of eatable fish an a given area.
