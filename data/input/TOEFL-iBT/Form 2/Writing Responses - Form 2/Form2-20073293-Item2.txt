	One can notice atempts of cooperation between people from the very beginings of human history. There is evidence that even the primitives cooperated in order to find food or fight with animals. In modern societies cooperation has also become a significant part. Whether in real life or in business people tend to come together in order to accomplish the difficult tasks they might have. 
	Some people often claim though, that in today's world, the ability to cooperate well with others is far more important than it was in the past. It is a fact that, the living circumstances were more difficult in the past. Indeed there were people who did not even have the money to eat or buy clothes. As a result they cooperated in order to find food, or the money needed to provide them with those human needs.
	However, it is of no less importance today's need for cooperation. Cooperation is an ability which can be developed. Therefore, when people need to work together in order to achieve their goals, they also develop their ability to cooperate. In modern societies we often notice people working together. In fact, we are trained to do so. In businesses or in school people are expected to have some important behavioural skills, including the ability to cooperate well with others.
	Take, for instance, the case of the educational system.
Students, whether in school or in university often have to do group assignments. They are encouraged, if not even required, to work together and bring a certain result. Sometimes those students have to meet in libraries or their houses in order to finish their homework.
	Taking everything into consideration and for all the above mentioned reasons and examples, I strongly believe that the ability to cooperate well with others is deliberately enhanced in today's world exactly because it is considered to be of great value.
