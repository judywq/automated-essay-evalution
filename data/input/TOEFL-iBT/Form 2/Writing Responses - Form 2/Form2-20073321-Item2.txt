The notion that in todays world, the ability to cooperate well with others is far more important than it was in the past is unfounded. Firstly lets define cooperation. Rudimentary understanding of the term would suggest definitions such as respect, understanding and getting along. Todays society has not changed much from the yeasteryears, albeit in has in some aspects such as technology. Cultures who hail from different corners of the globe all believe in the same universal notion of treating others like they want to be treated. Common courtesy and respect are universal traits.  Even in biblical times people believed in the axiom "do unto others as you would want them to do unto thee".
	As defined in the aforementioned paragraph cooperation can infer different meanings. Respect can be one of them and is a behavioral characteristic that varies from person to person, thus it is not restrictred to time periods as suggested in the question. Whether an individual today ought place more emphasis on cooperating well with others in comparison to an individual who was raised in the 1800's will vary based on the situation and not the time period. 
	Cooperation occurs when people of different beliefs and values come together for a common goal. In the yesteryears this occured when people travelled to far away lands and traded. Cooperation existed back then and was very important in establishing relationships between different cultures and societies. Expedition teams during the discovery of the new world faced immense challenges when meeting with natives for the first time. They were able to negotiate and trade goods and services by cooperating with each other and understanding each other.
	Therefore I do not agree with the notion that cooperation is far more important than it was in the past.
