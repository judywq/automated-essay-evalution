I don't think in today's world it's far more important to be able to cooperate with others than it used to be.

I think that today, as well as it was in the past, it is extremely important to have this ability. 

There are many situations in everyday life that are hard to face if you're completely on your own. 
Today a typical working environment consists of a team that cooperates to achieve a common goal. As an example I will use a small company. Somebody is responsibe for production, someone for bookkeeping and a third party for marketing. If these people work together in the most efficient way, the success of the company can be maximised. If there is one weak link in the chain, the productivity of the whole business will be damaged. 
You can have the most brilliant product that will not sell if there is no proper marketing.
Now let's have a look at society a few hundred years back. Most businesses were run by families. A farm was ran and kept alife by the whole family including husband and wife, children, sometimes also grandparents or more distant relatives. No single farmer would have been able to grow grain, transport it to the market and sell it on his own.
He needed the assistance of other workers to do so.

But today as well as in past times there are some professions in which the possibility of a one-person business exists.
A few hundred years ago that might have been a travelling musician or merchant. Today there are successfull small enterprises that are managed by a single person.

While today society has become much more interlinked and globalized, there is also more focus on the individual. Nontheless, as long as humans live together in villages, cities and countries, the ability to coorperate in business as well as in private life is one of the most important abilities to live a succesfull and satisfying life.
