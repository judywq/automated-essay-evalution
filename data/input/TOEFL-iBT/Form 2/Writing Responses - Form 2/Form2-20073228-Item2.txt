Nowadays, the incidence with which people interact with different cultures and mentalities is far greater than it used to be, let's say, fifty years ago. That is a function of globalisation and internet development.Obviously, in order to be able to cooperate efectivelly, people need to have good interpersonal skills that allow them to send the message accross, overcoming language barriers, age and cultural differences. 

I live in a foreign country and when I first moved here, I found it very difficult to talk to people. Not because of language issues, but because of the way I was brought up, in a former communist country. I wasn't used to talk too much, or give too many details of my life, or what I do. In time, I realised that I need to start being more friendly and communicate better with people, otherwise I will wake up one morning and realise I am lonely. I soon got a job at a non-profit organisation and since then I had learned a lot of things from my colleagues. The fact that it was an international environment made it easier for me to observe how different people behave and what is accepted or not. I learned what team work really means, as in my past experience in Romania it was considered a way for people to steal ideas and take credit for somebody's else work. I am now able to convey messages about all aspects of my work and I talk daily with people from around the world. We all work together, pursuing the same objectives,  we debate and argue a lot of issues, ranging from politics to favorite cooking recepies and most important, we are open to changes and ways of improving cooperation with our colleagues, friends and partners. 

To summarize, I think that in today's world, good cooperation with other people does make a bigger difference, both professionaly and personally. It has done wonders for me.
