In the lecture, the professor attacks the argument developped in the reading passage. Actually he desagrees with what is said in this text. 
So he makes his lecture on three points: defence of wild species of costal areas, use of chemical products in the farms and effect of domesticated fish food on human.
First he challenges the fact that wild local fish are endangered by the farms on the costal area. He explains that the farms are in fact protecting those areas where fishes are already endangered by traditionnal fishing by reducing the quantity of fish coming from traditionnal fishing.
Then he argues that food from farm fishes are obviously better than beef or pork, which are also farmed, based on the quantity of chemicals used to breed them. Moreover, they benefit from better nutritional values.
Eventually, he claims that even if fish farmers must use far more quantity of fish to produce one pound of farmed fish, they use unedible fish and so it is no a real waste of protein available from the sea.
