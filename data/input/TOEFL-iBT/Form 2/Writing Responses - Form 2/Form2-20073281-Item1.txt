Since its  introduction  in the 1960s  fish farming has always led to dicussions about its possible risks and benefits.
In contrary to the text, the professor is in favour of fish farming for several reasons:
The article states that fish farming endageres local wild fish population by bringing in an increased risk of infections and diseases. However, the professor states that wild fish populations have already been  endangered  by overfishing long before fish farming was introduced. Therefore, he believes, that wild fish population could even regenerate by taking the fishing pressure away from them.
Another point the article mentions is the use of growth-inducing chemicals which might be a health risk for consumers.
However, according to the professor this is not a major problem. Of course, if wild fish is compared to farmed fish, there will be a higher concentration of potentially harming substances. But if it is compared to meat, etc., fish, which has been raised in farms, is very healthy, because of its low fat and high nutritional value.
The last point in the article states that a high amount of fish is just produced in order to feed the farmed fish. According to the professor, however, only fish that cannot be eaten by humans is used. So, in his opinion, only eatable fish is raised in bigger amounts.
Alltogether, the professor believes in fish farming to be an attrctive alternative to capturing wild fish.
