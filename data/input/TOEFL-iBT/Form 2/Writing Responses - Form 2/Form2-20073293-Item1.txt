	In the lecture the professor challenges the first point made in the reading passage by saying that the wild fish, that is presumably endangered by fish farming, is already in danger due to regular fishing. In addition to this, he states that farm fishing is a better alternative to regular fishing.
	Secondly, the professor claims that people should prefer eating farm-raised fish than, for example, poultry, beef, or pork. Regardless to the fact that those fish contain chemicals, which are harmful to the man, they also have less fat and better nutritional value than the above mentioned sources of food. Besides, all food people eat nowadays is filled with chemicals.
	Furthermore, regarding the wastefulness of the fish farming process, the professor supports that species of fish that are used to feed the farm-raised fish are usually not eaten by people. Thus, the fish farming process indirectly makes people eat fish, that normally is of no feeding use to people.
