     The topic of both the reading passage and the lecture is fish farming, which means the production of the fish in the coastal area. The professor suggests solutions of each three problems of fish farming that are mentioned in the passage.
     The first difficulty is the danger of the wild fish around the farming area, according to the author. He says that epidemics or parasites can affect local fish, then wild fish could be endangered. However, the professor argues that the wild fish which lives near farming area are frequently already endangered. He says that there are more traditional factors than reasons caused by fish farming.
     Next, the author concerns about chemicals used to enlarge the size of the fish. He asserts that the effect human would receive has not been known. On the other hand, according to the lecturer, most of those chemicals are edible to humans. The professor also argues that farmer also know that the health of the fish is more important than their weight or height.
     Finally, in opinion of the author, the fish farming is not so effecient that the farmers use fish meal, and the degree of fish meal overweighs the quantity of the raised fish. However, the speaker claims that although some of fish are fed with fish meal, but majority of foods fish eat are made by inedible sources. Thus, the professor thinks that there is no wastes on fish farming.
