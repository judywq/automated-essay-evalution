  Actually the lecturer doesn't agree with the statements made in the reading. There are three main problems emphasised.
   The first is that the farm made fish is jeopardizing the wild fish. The problem is that when there are a lot of fish in a closed area then this increases the chance of diseases. The farmers are using medicines for their own fish but this is still a danger for the wild fish living around the farm. The lecturer said that this isn't exactly true because in the places where the fish farms are usually build the wild fish is already  endangered. The farming is just an alternative to the fishing of wild fish.
   The second problem is that the fish is usually fed with fish growth-inducing chemicals. The lecturer is arguing that similar chemicals are used in the production of other types of meat like pork and beef. In the same time the consumption of fish is less harmful to humans than the other types of meat because it contains less fat and better nutritions. In this way the eating of fish is in favour of our health instead of in harm of it. 
   The last problem is that the fish farms are wasteful. The lecturer doesn't agree again. He said that the effectiveness of the fish production can be increased and this is not a reason not to produce fish in farms.
