I agree with this statement that in today's world, the ability to cooperate well with others is far more important than it was in the past. It is one of the essential ability for us to survive in today's society.
First, we can make impossible possible by working together.  Things in today's world are much more complicated that it was in the past. Take science as an example, Newton Three Laws for Motion cost Newton's whole life. But in today's highschool, we only spend two weeks to learn these three laws and how to use them to solve real life problems. The fact is that most of the  "simple" stuff have already been foundand or already been done. It is nearly impossible to make an difference in the world by working alone.  If we want to discover some new laws or new theories that other people have never found before or do some really new things that other people have never never done before, we have to work with others.  One people only has limited talent, but when a lot of brilliant minds meet together, any thing can happen. Just like when Bill and Paul got together, Microsoft was born.
Second, we can learn new things that we do not know by working with others. Although we can know and learn nearly everything in this world by reading books, searching internet. But those methods are acually another way of cooperating with others because it is their knowledge. It is a way for those people to show their willingness to work with us. So, we can see that actually there is only one way of learning and it is to cooperate with others. We can learn form other people and others people can  learn from us. In this way can we improve ourselves.
That is why I believe the ability to cooperate well with others is a basic skill for us in today's world.
