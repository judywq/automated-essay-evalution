The first point of the lecture is that in costal areas the local fish population is endangered by the traditional commercial fishing more than it is by the illnesses that fish farming could produce. In addition wild fish could benefit from the reduction in commercial fishing that the fish farming produce.
The second point states that even though growth-inducing chemicals are used in fish farming, such chemicals are also used in other kinds of farming, like in the production of beef and pork meat. But fish contains better nutritional elements than meat does; therefore it is surely healthier to eat farmed fish than meat.
In his last point the lecturer challenges the long-term wastefulness of fish farming. He says that the most part of the fish meal is made by processing ineatable fish. Therefore fish farming transform ineatable fish in eatable fish.
