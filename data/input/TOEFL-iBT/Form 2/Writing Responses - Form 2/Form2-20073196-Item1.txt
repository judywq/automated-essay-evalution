	The lecture emphasizes the difference between the aspects shown in the reading and what really happens according to the lecturer. First, the Professor states that wild fish are not endangered because of fish farms. On the contrary, he mentions commercial fishing as the leading cause of endangering wild fish life and defends fish farms as a means of maintaining the fish population stable. Second, the Professor disagrees on the risk presented by the feeding of growth inducing chemicals to fish. He uses the example of beef, poultry, and pork and explains that these animals are also chemically grown and are consumed even more than fish. In order to defend the nutritious advantages of fish he says that fish have less fat than the other three foods. Finally the lecturer tells us that the species utilized to process fish meal are not the species of fish eaten by human beings. He mentions that in order to contrast the view implied in the reading which says that the more fish meal produced, the less protein available from the sea.
