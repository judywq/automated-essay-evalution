Hatcheries (fish farms) considered one of the means of protecting endangered fish such as Salmon, however; these means are not free from disadvantages. The following three cons are associated with them: 

These hatcheries putting the health of the wild fish in the surroundings of the hatcheries in risk, especially when the ones that are inside the farms being confined into a small area, they might get infested with parasitic infections, which in turn have the capability of being transported to the above-mentioned wild counterparts. Another disadvantage would be that, given the fact that these fish in the farms being grew up on different chemicals, they might pose a risk on consumer's health, especially on long-term basis. The third con based on the argument that these fish in the hatcheries require a large amount of food, which might has a detrimental effect on the amount of protein in the sea.

The professor on the other hand has argued these claims by raising the issue of consuming fish by human beings is growing and he pointed out the importance of comparing consumption rate of fish with other types of food products instead of comparing the hatcheries with the wild fish environment. 

Additionally, he has shed the light on the reality that these fish farms has an advantage that is clearly has both a healthy and an economic outcomes, which is, they increase the number of edible fish and that was clear when the professor has galvanized that about one-third of the fish on the market now adays is from hatcheries.
