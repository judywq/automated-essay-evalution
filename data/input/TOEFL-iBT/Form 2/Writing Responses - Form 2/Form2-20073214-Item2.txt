If one looks back upon the history of the world, it becomes clear that the way humans interact and cooperate has changed very much up until the current day. 
Because "the past" is a very vague indication of time, I will take the liberty of going as far back as the stone age. Back in those days most of the world population was on their own. There were no big cities, no huge communities whatsoever. One would generally find small groups of people or scattered families. The fact that these early humans didn't have a highly devoloped language proves that the necissity of cooperating and communication didn't really exist at that time. Over the centuries, different cultures developed different languages and larger communities and cities began to form. The word cooperation started to gain importance. When naval explorations led to mans expansion across the globe, international relations began to play an important role. 
The word "globalization", an expression which has its roots in the 20th century implies cross cultural communication and cooperation. Today more than ever do we find the need for this sort of cooperation. If you think of the business world, nobody can deny that international relations play an extremely important role. In this field, the ability to cooperate with others has the highest priority. If you lack cultural empathy and the ability to change your point of view, you are lost. For instance if you fail to reckognize the needs of your business partner or can't find an agreement on a subject, you will eventually fail. Of course this is only one example of the many situations in which the ability to cooperate with others is essential.  It is also clear that every human could survive without the ability of cooperating with others. But it can be seen as a law of physics that in today's world underlying the principle of globalization, only the ones that cooperate with others in harmony can be successful in life.
