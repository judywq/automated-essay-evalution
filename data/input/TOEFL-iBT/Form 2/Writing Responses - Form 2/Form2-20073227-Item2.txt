Cooperation is, and has always been an important attribute of humans and creatures on this planet. If the bees did not help the flowers spread their pollen, there wouldn't be any flowers. And if the flowers did not have juice for the bees then we wouldn't have any bees. To claim that cooperation was not important in the past is to deny the cooperation of our nature and its inhabitants.
The statement is false in several aspects of life such as work and career, politics and social life.

First, cooperation at work and with the people around you is today as important as it was in the past. For people in the stone age, for example, cooperation must have been very important when they went hunting, fishing or even when they prepared food or built shelters. Surely, they were all depending on their ability to work as a team. Just as we are today in almost every workplace you could name.

Secondly, the governments and entities that governed the people in the past must have been working well together or else the people would not follow their leaders. A leader who could provide for his  people and satisfy their many needs, must have had good cooperational skills to reach his position. It is the same today, we love or at least accept many of our politicians and leaders because they do mostly good things for us. In some countries however, the leaders are not cooperating with the people and as a result those countries are often unstable.

Finally, in our social life and that of the people who lived in the past,  cooperation was always present. Peoples ability to cooperate helps them find a mate, friends and to resolve difficult situations arising from some problem. 

If cooperating with each other in the past was not important, then it would surely not be important now. Besides, even animals can cooperate.
