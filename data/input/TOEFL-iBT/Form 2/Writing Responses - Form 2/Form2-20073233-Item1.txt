The lecture points out the different arguments used in the reading passage against fish-farming. According to the reading passage, fish-farming presents several negative aspects but, in the lecture the professor explains why all those arguments are not so negative and even sometimes become positive points.
the fist point in the lecture is the influence of fish farming on the costal areas. According to the professor, the species in the costal areas are already in danger because of fishing and actually, costal fish-farming may refresh the surrounding wild fishes  with new specimens from those farms.
The second point is the risk to human health. It is said in the text that fishes are fed with chemical foods, and then those chemical substances come into the human body. According to the professor the chemical substances are the same for meat like (beef) so it's not something new and specific from that process. Actually fish contain less fat and more proteins than meat, so according to the professor it is still healthier to eat fish.
The last point is, contrary to what it is said in the text (that fish-farming diminishes the protein available from the sea because some fishes are used to feed the fishes in the farm), fish-farming increases the amount of proteins from the sea to the consumer, because the fishes used to feed the fishes in the farm are not used as human food then instead being totally useless (I am talking about feeding people) they take a part in the rocesse of growing fish, then they become useful and increases the amount of proteins taken from the sea and used for feeding people.
