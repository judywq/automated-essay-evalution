The lecturer challenges each of the arguments used in the reading passage. First, he says that the fish populations that could be endangered by fish farming usually already are seriously theatened by conventional fishing. He therefore sees fish farming as a good alternative which, by reducing the amount of fish harvested by fishermen, helps the endangered fish populations to rebound.
The lecturer then criticizes the claim that farm-raised fish may be dangerous for human health. He says that although it is true that several different chemichals are used to raise the fish, all the other kinds of food people buy also contain such chemicals. Therefore the health consequences of eating farm-fish should be compared to those of eating other types of food such as beef or lamb. From this point of view, fish is a better choice because it contains less fat and is generally healthier than the other types of meat that people usually eat.
Finally, the lecturer explains that although fish meal is often used by farmers to feed their fish, the claim that this technique reduces the amount of protein available from the sea is irrelevant. According to him, what counts is the population of eatable fish, and since fish meal is made from fish that humans usually do not eat, there is no harm done there.
