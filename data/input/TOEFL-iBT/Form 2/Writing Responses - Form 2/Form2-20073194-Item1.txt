There were three major points in the lecture that challenge the three points made in the reading. The first point was about the fact that not only fish farming does not harm the health of wild fish with the development of diseases and parasitic infections, but on the contrary it helps preserve the wild fish population. The wild fish population close to the shoreline was already endangered by the commersial fishing. Nowadays, fish farming provides an alternative to fishing wild fish, thus helping preserve it. 
The second argument, stated that even though there are growth-inducing chemicals used, this should not be considered as a reason against fish farming as such chemicals are also used in raising paultry, beef and pork consumed by people more frequently than fish. Instead the lecturer proposes that we should take into consideration the benefits of consuming fish, like the fact that fish provides us with more nutrients and less fat. 
The final point he has made is that not only the fact that some fish is used in fish meals to feed the farm fish is not a waste, but it is in fact turning fish that is not eatable, into food for the fish normally consumed by people. Therefore the reading's third argument against fish farming does not constitute a reason to stop farming, too.
