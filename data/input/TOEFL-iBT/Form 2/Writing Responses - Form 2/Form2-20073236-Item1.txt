      Fish farming is increasingly becoming more common than the traditional fishing method in meeting the increasing demand for fish production. Just like any other thing it has both negative and possitive impact on the environment and  the society. The lecture presented three points in rebutting the negative effects of the fish farming mentioned in the reading passage. These are: the possibility of endangering the health of wild sea animals within the area of farming; the health issues related to the consumption of fishes produced in the farm; and the ineffeciency of the feed conversion ratio in fish farming which competes with the available protein food from the sea.
     With regards to the health risk posed by fish farming, the lecture rebutted this  by saying that the depletion of the wild species was not the effect of the increasing number of fish diseases or parasites but by the unregulated fishing practices or methods used by the people.
    For the second point, the lecturer contend that farm fishing pose not as a potential health hazard to the consumer but rather it provide a healthier options for the people to consume less red meat and less fat from farmed fishes.
     The last point was negated by the lecture because the fish meal uses the inedible fish meats rather than the edible one consumed by the people. With this, the contention that fish farming reduces the  food available from the sea could not be possible but rather should improve.
     Therefore the lecture concluded that possitive impact could outweighs the negative impact posed by fish farming.
