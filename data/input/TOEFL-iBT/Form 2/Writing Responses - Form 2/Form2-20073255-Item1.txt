	The lecture points out that the problems mentioned in the reading passage can be solved. The passage is about the problems with the fish farming. There are three problems in the passage.
	First problem is the wild fishes being endangered. As the wild fish are confined to small area, the rate of diseases and parastic infections grow. According to the professor, those wild fish were already being endangered without human activity. The second problem is the chemicals. The chemicals are used by farmers to grow their fish bigger. The professor mentioned beef and pork, which are also fed with the chemicals. He said since the fish have less fat, it is a good thing to have a big fish. The last problem is the population of the wild fish is declining because they are eaten by farm-raised fish. The professor pointed out that the farm-raised fish are wating the fish which we don't eat.
