There are many advantages and disadvantages of fish farming and  many positions about the topic. 
	On one side, those who oppose to this pactice argue that fish farming is harming local fish populations and contaminating them with several diseases. On the other hand, those supporting fish farming point out that local populations have already been endangered long ago by regular local fishing. They insist that it is more important to take in consideration the benefits of this practice rather than the possible negative consequences.
	Moreover, oppositors suggest that the chemicals used in fish farming can cause severe negative health effects on consumers in the long-term. Although, there is no strong evidence in the present that support that statement. In contrast to that, there are people who make an emphasis on the consumer's benefits of fish consumption such as less fat and many nutrients which are important for the human body. They also state that chemicals are not only in fish meat but in other types of food that the population consume regularly. Those foods can be more harmful for humans than meat from farm fish.
	Another argument against fish farming relies on the fact that to feed farm fish, farmers used fish meals that are produced with processed wild fish which causes a decrease in the wild fish population. Hoewever, many state that such fish meals are produced with non eatable fish and that the production of fish in farms is increasing the number of eatable fish available for consumers.
	There are many arguments in favor and against fish farming, however, it  is the consumer who should learn about both positions and make the final decision.
