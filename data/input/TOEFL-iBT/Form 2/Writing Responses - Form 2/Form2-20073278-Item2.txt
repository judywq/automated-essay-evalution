	I disagree with the statement that the ability to cooperate well with others is much more important than it was in the past.  I have three points, the system of evaluation, making a dicision, and things that are unchanged from the past to the present.
	First, the type of employment and evaluation has changed.  In the past, people were evaluated as a menber of groups, so the group acheived something, the members could get the profit.  But today, when individualism prevailed all over the world, the evaluation is done to each employee personally, so people have to work hard by themselves to make profits.  It is very ideal to cooperate with others and achieve something together, but it is not realistic.  
	Second, with pervasive individualism, our own dicisions are more important than past.  It is natural that people want help from someone when they are sad, but we have to realize that each of us must live strongly in each life.  I noticed that recently.  When I passed some entrance exams of universities last year, I could not decide which school to go at first.  My friends and relatives gave me a lot of advice, but eventually I made up my mind to enter my current university.  What I have learned is that a decision is made by one person.  Now making dicision by yourself is more significant than cooperating with others.
	Finally, and most importantly,  cooperating with others is one of the most necessary skills in our lives, and we have to take it as granted.  The necessity and importance is unchanged, so in the terms of cooperation, we should not compare the past and the present.  In that sense, I disagree with the statement.
	In my conclusion, the phrase "the ability to cooperate well with others is more important than it was in the past" is wrong, but the ability itself is always important and it is necessary for everyone to learn the ability well.
