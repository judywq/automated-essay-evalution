Fish farming is the commercial rapid growing of fish along shorelines. The passage describes it as a recless and harmfull enterprise that basically contains three negativ points. 
First the fact that an unusally high number of fish is packed on relatively small area. This fact may result in an higher number of parasitic infections and diseases which could easyly spread to the wild fish living outside the farming area. 
To prevent this infections farmers use chemicals which may have an unheatly effect on the humans who will eat the fish. 
As a last point the text says that as an other result of the high amount of fish on an relatatively small area the farmers have to feed the fish. To do that they use fish meal what is a product of wild fish. 
The lecture on the other hand refutes all this points. Argumenting that the fish population in shore areas is endangered by classic fishing anyway. 
Chemicals are used in any kind of farming. Beef, pork and even agricultural products. But fish still helps the human with its excellent and healthy nutrition facts.
Last but not least the lecture says that fish meal is not eaten by humans anyway. So fish farmers are using a product that would be wasted if they would not use it. Both--the lecture and the passage-- have strong arguments but the lecture challenges those made in the text with better ones.
