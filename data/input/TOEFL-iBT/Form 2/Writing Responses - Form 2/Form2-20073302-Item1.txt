As the reading passage points out, fish farming has so many disadvantages that it should be abandoned. However, the lecturer casts doubt on the arguments of the author of the passage.
       First, the author thinks fish farming causes infectious diseases to spread among wild fish. As a matter of fact, the decrease in the number of wild fish is largely due to traditional commercial fishing instead of fish farming. Through fish farming, traditional commercial fishing is given up, and the number of wild fish makes a rebound.
       Second, fish farming is dangerous to human health as shown in the article. It points out that too many chemicals are used, but the lecturer tells us to be realistic about the use of chemicals. The poultry, beef, and pork we consume everyday all contains many chemicals, and what we should do is to compare the value of fish with the above-mentioned kinds of food rather than being fightened of the use of chemicals in fish farming. Fish is much nutritious so eating fish still does us benefits even though fish also contains some chemicals.
        Third, it is said by the author that fish farming causes wastefulness in that too much fish meal is consumed in the process. Nonetheless, fish meal is made up of the kinds of fish that are inedible, and through changing these fish into fish meal used in fish farming, they become edible. Consequently, fish farming is a good example of making good use of resources instead of wastefulness.
